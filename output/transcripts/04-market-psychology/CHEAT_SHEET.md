# Market Psychology — Cheat Sheet

## Why Markets Are Predictably Irrational

- Market cycles repeat every ~4 years because human cognitive biases are universal and unchanging
- Price moves in **anticipation** of future events, not in response to them — "you make money doing today what people will recognize tomorrow"
- "Buy the rumor, sell the news" — smart money buys before announcements and sells when news becomes public
- Markets move in the direction that hurts the most people — designed to shake you out of good positions before the move happens

## The Seven Cognitive Biases to Kill

| Bias | What It Does | Antidote |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Confirmation bias | Seek info that confirms existing beliefs | Actively look for reasons you are wrong |
| Bandwagon effect | Copy the herd | Do the opposite of the crowd at extremes |
| Anchoring | Cling to the first price you heard | Re-evaluate independently at all times |
| Hindsight bias | "I knew that would happen" — false competence | If you didn't buy, there's a flaw in your system — fix it |
| Deprival super-reaction | Refuse to sell at a loss | Accept the loss early; thesis invalidation overrides ego |
| Inconsistency avoidance | Resist changing your view | Update beliefs when evidence changes |
| Doubt avoidance | Ignore bad news on holdings | Actively seek and investigate negative information |

## Pain Is the Signal

- Buying when it feels **painful** = good entry (low prices, high fear, strong future)
- Buying when it feels **easy** = bad entry (high prices, high confidence, weak future)
- "Buying fear is scary upfront and peaceful later. Buying greed is easy upfront and scary later."
- The more painful a buying decision feels, the better the long-term setup

## Contrarian Rules

- Be contrarian **only at extremes** (tops and bottoms) — in the middle of a trend, follow the trend
- "The trend is your friend" applies mid-bull market; contrarianism applies at peaks and bottoms
- At the bottom: everyone has sold → cash holders vastly outnumber coin holders → only direction is up
- At the top: everyone is confident and buying → only direction is down
- "Dead people are some of the best investors" — they can't react to market noise and shake themselves out

## Regret and System Thinking

- When you feel regret about a missed trade, do not ask "how do I get into this coin?" — ask "where did my system fail?"
- Fix the root cause (system flaw), not the individual instance
- "If you have a system, you never say 'I regret not buying' because the system didn't find the coin"
- Do not attach your identity to a coin (e.g., putting its name in your bio) — it makes critical thinking impossible

## The Goal: 80% of Gains, 100% of the Time

- Perfection is impossible in markets — regret is inevitable
- The goal is "consistently good enough": capture roughly 80% of possible gains 100% of the time
- Attempting to catch every top and bottom perfectly results in missing entirely
- "The most profitable strategy is to face pain upfront, consider all risks before entry, and then do nothing"

## Key Rules

1. Identify your cognitive bias when you feel it — then do the opposite
2. "In markets you get paid for doing the opposite of what your instincts tell you"
3. Do not counter-trade an ongoing bull trend ("the market can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent")
4. Look for futures the market has not yet priced in — that is where the opportunity lives
5. Always ask the downside first: "What could go wrong? How would I lose?"
6. Pre-commit to your decisions before entering high-stress situations — decide what you will do in each scenario before it happens
