# How Markets Price Value

**Section:** Market Psychology
**Loom ID:** d3bcec40b8a84bb29dd15882276e1db4

## Key Takeaways

- Markets are forward-looking — price reflects what participants expect to happen in the future, not what the asset is worth today; you make money by doing today what people will recognize tomorrow.
- "Buy the rumor, sell the news" — smart investors buy before the announcement and sell days before it comes out, leaving late buyers to experience the post-news dump.
- Markets are not a fair reflection of value because of cognitive biases, emotional decision-making, and recency bias (projecting recent trends to continue linearly).
- The market can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent — trying to counter-trade a bull trend mid-cycle is dangerous even if you are fundamentally correct.
- In a zero-sum closed room model: when everyone has sold into cash (maximum fear) is the best time to hold coins; when everyone holds coins (maximum greed) is the time to be in cash.
- Being contrarian only works at extremes — in the middle of a bull trend, the trend is your friend; only at the end of euphoria or end of despair does counter-trend pay.
