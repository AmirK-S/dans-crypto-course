# Ignore Regret

**Section:** Market Psychology
**Loom ID:** 97531dc99d9b4e3aabe7715f1b6e0a80

## Key Takeaways

- Regret is inevitable in markets — every single investor could have made more money, so you must accept and ignore regret rather than be paralyzed by it.
- The key to market success is not perfection but being "consistently good enough" — maximizing for making 80% of what you can, 100% of the time.
- When you feel regret about a missed coin, ask the right question: not "how do I get into this coin?" but "where did my system fail and how do I fix it?"
- Regret assumes you could have caught the opportunity — if you have a proper system, you accept that the system won't catch everything, but it will catch enough to make you wealthy.
- The experience of regret reveals you have no system — a system-driven investor never says "I regret not buying" because the system either found the coin or didn't.
- Fix the root cause of the mistake (pull the weed from the root) rather than trying to fix the one missed instance — the latter is drinking to cure depression.
