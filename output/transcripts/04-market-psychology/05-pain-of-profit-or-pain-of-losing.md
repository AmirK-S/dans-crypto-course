# Pain of profit or pain of losing?

**Section:** Market Psychology
**Loom ID:** 4dfec1dcb97148319b8b0174b851ef22

## Key Takeaways

- When you buy something in markets you will always feel pain — the choice is whether to feel the pain upfront (buying when it hurts, setting up for profit) or later (buying when it feels good, then watching it drop).
- Buying during fear is scary upfront and peaceful later; buying during greed is easy upfront and scary later.
- By the time you really feel like buying (near the top), most of the upside is already priced in and price will need to go lower in the next fear cycle.
- The market moves in the direction that hurts the most people — if everyone bought at one price, it will move below that price to shake them out before going higher.
- Dead people are some of the best investors because the market cannot trick them into participating at the wrong time — minimizing unnecessary participation is a key to long-term success.
- Before entering a long-term investment, mentally consider 50 different what-if scenarios — this front-loading of thinking reduces emotional decision-making once you're in the position.
