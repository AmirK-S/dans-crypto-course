# All humans have the same flaws

**Section:** Market Psychology
**Loom ID:** 183dcce19016485596a81932485930cd

## Key Takeaways

- Market cycles repeat because all humans are born with the same pre-downloaded cognitive biases — confirmation bias, bandwagon effect, anchoring, and hindsight bias — and these biases never go away.
- To make money in markets you must identify your cognitive biases, recognize when you're feeling them, and do the opposite of what they push you toward.
- Bandwagon effect means buying when sentiment is most negative is often the best trade — when no one is left to sell, the only direction is up.
- Anchoring bias makes it hard to change beliefs once formed — people who believe "we're in a bear market" keep believing it even as the bull run begins, missing the best entry.
- Hindsight bias ("I knew I should have bought") creates false competence — if you didn't buy, you failed and must fix your system, not console yourself with almost-buying.
- Market cycles take three to four years specifically because that's how long it takes for people to forget the lesson they learned — they repeat the same mistakes every cycle.
