# Don’t mix strategies

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** a9b7500b390c4ee289ecfaa366e3957a

## Key Takeaways

- Each strategy in crypto (long-term investing, momentum trading, day trading) has completely different entry rules — mixing them creates a Frankenstein approach that has none of the advantages of any strategy and all of the downsides of each.
- Long-term investing: buy in the fear zone only, no stop-loss, hold until thesis plays out or is invalidated, entry must be asymmetric. Momentum trading: buy breakouts with a stop-loss, capture a defined move, exit within weeks. Day trading: any price level is fine with a tight stop-loss and take profit set in advance.
- The most common mixing mistake: buying a breakout (trader entry) but then holding indefinitely without a stop-loss (investor behavior) — now you have asymmetric downside with no risk management.
- The reverse mistake: buying during fear (investor entry) but selling at the first sign of momentum (trader exit) — you get out right before the big move that justified the painful entry in the first place.
- Letting a losing trade become an investment is one of the clearest signs of having no strategy — if a swing trade goes against you and you "just hold it," you converted a momentum position into a buy-at-peak-greed long-term investment.
- Be explicit about your strategy before entering every position — state whether this is a long-term investment or a trade, and then apply exclusively that strategy's rules from entry to exit.
