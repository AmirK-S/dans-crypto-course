# Gain conviction with higher prices

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** 472c1d6853b645c08411ef54df6a4e8c

## Key Takeaways

- As an asset's price rises, upside becomes less asymmetric — your interest in buying should decrease as the coin goes higher, not increase; if it's already 3x, your core position should already be filled.
- Adding more capital late in a bull run raises your cost basis to the point where even early winners become losers — buy near the bottom with all available capital, then reduce risk as prices climb higher.
- "Buying when it feels right is buying at a premium" — comfort costs you money because the consensus that something is a good buy is itself a sign that the asymmetric opportunity has passed.
- The two pains of crypto: buy when it hurts and suffer short-term discomfort for long-term gain, or buy when it feels good and suffer long-term pain when price reverts — there is no comfortable path to profit.
- This lesson applies specifically to long-term investors — momentum traders and day traders with pre-set stop losses can legitimately buy breakouts because their downside is capped mechanically.
- Long-term investing is primarily a psychological game: the entry timing is about sentiment analysis (buying fear, selling euphoria), while fundamental analysis defines what to buy but not when.
