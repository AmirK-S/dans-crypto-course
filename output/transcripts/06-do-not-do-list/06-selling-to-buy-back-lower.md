# Selling to buy back lower

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** 6c3c770bf0ed42fd864d7eee87e93e20

## Key Takeaways

- The brain generates the idea to "sell and buy back lower" as a pain-mitigation response — this idea always arrives at the worst possible time: near the bottom, right when the urge to sell is highest and the rebound is imminent.
- Winners don't give you re-entries — if you own a winning coin and sell expecting a dip, the dip is often tiny (13-25%) and price immediately continues higher, leaving you forced to chase it at a worse price or miss the move entirely.
- Each additional decision you add to a long-term position creates new risk — you have to get 100 things right instead of two, reducing the probability of a successful outcome.
- The Big Money is made from the main movement, not individual fluctuations — buying Solana once at $20 and holding to $200+ creates far more wealth than constantly trading in and out trying to optimize entries.
- Selling to buy lower also increases taxes in most countries — short-term capital gains rates can be significantly higher than long-term rates, often eliminating any gains from the attempted re-entry.
- "Selling to buy back lower is trader brain" — unless you are a consistently profitable trader, any successful re-entry is luck, and relying on luck over 20 years is guaranteed to destroy wealth.
