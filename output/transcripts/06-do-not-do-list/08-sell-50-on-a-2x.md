# Sell 50% on a 2x

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** d73f1c6530ca4ee0ac5fc9a5771aa8fb

## Key Takeaways

- Selling half on a 2x destroys home run potential — if a coin is a 30x winner and you sold half at 2x, you only ever capture the equivalent of a 15x, losing the upside that was the entire point of the investment.
- The only three valid reasons to sell a position: you were right (thesis played out), you were wrong (thesis invalidated), or you found something better (opportunity cost) — "it doubled" is not one of these.
- Selling half on a 2x leads to portfolio dilution: the freed capital gets deployed into more coins, spreading attention too thin to track any of them well, resulting in standardized market returns at best.
- "Winners tend to keep winning" — if a coin has gone 2x, it's emerging as a winner in a market where most coins barely move; selling the winner to reduce risk eliminates the only position likely to continue outperforming.
- Selling half on a 2x creates casino mentality ("playing with house money") — viewing portfolio gains as "free money" leads to less disciplined decision-making and ultimately to losing those gains.
- Know your exit liquidity before you buy: identify exactly who will buy your coin at 10x-50x (their background, goals, stage in crypto journey) — if you can visualize this person clearly, you know your thesis and when to exit without needing arbitrary rules.
