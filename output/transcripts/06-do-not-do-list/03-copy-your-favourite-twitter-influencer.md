# Copy your favourite twitter influencer

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** 54d2f81553cd410ab09a918fe9cff00a

## Key Takeaways

- Influencer conviction is highest on coins that have already pumped — the coin they're most confident about is usually the worst performer going forward, while the coin they barely mention often outperforms everything.
- Twitter and YouTube are coordinated shill machines: influencers accumulate bags, then coordinate public posts to create FOMO buyers who become their exit liquidity — they never tell you when to sell.
- It's not the coin that makes you rich, it's the process — even the best coin call with no strategy (no exit, no thesis, no risk management) will end in losing money once the position inevitably gets tested.
- The 20-year return of copying Twitter influencers is zero because you're borrowing temporary profits from smart money that will eventually be reclaimed when the market asks for it back.
- If you must follow someone, follow them precisely (entry AND exit, in real-time alongside them) in a paid group context — not secondhand Twitter calls where you're always getting inferior entries and never getting exits.
- The right approach: build your own system, find coins independently, run ideas by your community for feedback, but make your own decisions based on your own thesis — internal to external, not external to internal.
