# Buy or sell without an edge

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** 501889370f0c4dcca6a8a3ecdcf3e0c1

## Key Takeaways

- Without an edge, you are statistically destined to lose money over time — like a slot machine returning 90 cents per dollar, every edgeless decision erodes capital until it reaches zero.
- Everyone makes money in a bull run without an edge, but they cannot keep it — "temporary profit" comes from borrowing money from smart players who will eventually take it back when the market corrects.
- Markets are zero-sum: every buy has a seller who thinks they are correct — your job is to have a better thesis than the person on the other side of your trade, not just to make one good punch.
- Questions to verify you have an edge before buying or selling: Does this decision hurt? Am I doing the hard thing or the easy thing? Is this logic or emotion? Would a professional I admire make this decision?
- The goal is not to avoid losing — it's to make losses small (cut below the red line) and let wins be large (asymmetric upside) — a profitable edge is one where gains consistently outweigh losses over time.
- Crypto is like a soccer match, not a boxing punch: you need a game plan, a strategy, team structure, and a coach (mentor) — running around like a headless chicken copying others guarantees you lose the match.
