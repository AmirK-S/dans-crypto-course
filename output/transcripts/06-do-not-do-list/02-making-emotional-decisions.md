# Making emotional decisions

**Section:** Do-NOT-Do-List
**Loom ID:** 5e72de832fb3498697f62c220a742b8a

## Key Takeaways

- Emotions are instincts you're born with — they were useful for survival but in markets, acting on them means buying tops and selling bottoms, the exact opposite of what makes money.
- The most powerful use of emotions: recognizing when a decision feels painful means it's likely the correct one — buying should hurt, selling should hurt; if it feels good, you're probably wrong.
- Proven strategies and systems (algorithms) remove emotion from decision-making by replacing it with data-driven logic — this is the primary tool for becoming a consistently profitable investor.
- Invest slightly more than what feels emotionally comfortable — the amount that feels "safe" to invest is never enough to materially change your financial situation.
- Being too busy looking at charts all day is a guaranteed way to make worse decisions — "the best stock portfolios are of dead people" because they can't make emotional decisions.
- Listen to yourself first, others last — but only after becoming someone worth listening to through building actual knowledge and a repeatable investment process.
