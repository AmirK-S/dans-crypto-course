# Do Not Do List — Cheat Sheet

## The Three Valid Reasons to Sell

1. **You were right** — the thesis played out as expected
2. **You were wrong** — the thesis has been invalidated
3. **You found something better** — a higher-conviction opportunity with more asymmetric upside

Any sell outside these three reasons is likely emotional and will hurt returns.

## The Emotion Test (Before Every Buy or Sell)

Ask yourself before acting:
- Does this hurt to do? (If buying: good sign. If selling: good sign.)
- Am I doing the easy thing or the hard thing?
- Is this based on logic or emotion?
- Could I imagine a successful professional making this exact decision?
- Who has the better thesis — the buyer or the seller?

"You have to buy when it hurts and you have to sell when it hurts."

## Things You Must Never Do

| What | Why It's Wrong |
|------|---------------|
| Chase a pump based on emotion | Worst coins pump last — you're selling your winner to buy a dud |
| Copy Twitter/YouTube influencers | They tell you when to buy, never when to sell — you are their exit liquidity |
| Gain conviction as price rises | Asymmetric upside only exists near the bottom — conviction should decrease as price rises |
| Buy/sell without an edge | Averaged over 20 years, edge-less decisions return zero or negative |
| Sell to buy back lower | Winning coins will not give you a re-entry — you'll end up in laggards |
| Mix strategies | Long-term investing + momentum trading = guaranteed losses |
| Sell half on a 2x | Cuts the upside of home run investments; not real risk management |
| Look at charts all day | "You make the most money by doing nothing" |
| Add capital late in the bull run | Raises cost basis; destroys the asymmetry that made you profitable |
| Follow more than 1-2 strategies/influencers | Conflicting signals across strategies produces net losses |

## Selling Half on a 2x: Why It's Wrong

- The key to crypto is **asymmetric returns**: risk a little to make a lot
- Cutting winners early violates the asymmetric return principle
- Creates "playing with house money" psychology → leads to gambling behavior
- Increases position count → harder to time individual exits at the top
- "Winners tend to keep winning" — the home run position is the one you cut early

## Mixing Strategies

Each strategy has **incompatible rules**:

| Strategy | Entry | Stop Loss | Exit |
|----------|-------|-----------|------|
| Long-term investing | Buy during fear | Thesis invalidation | Thesis plays out |
| Momentum trading | Buy breakouts | Percentage stop loss | Technical resistance or trend break |
| Day trading | Technical setups | Preset stop loss | Preset take profit |

"Letting your trades that go wrong become investments is a surefire sign of a lack of strategy."

## Valid Trigger to Shift Capital Between Coins

Only shift from one coin to another if you can answer YES to all three:
1. Does your preferred coin have more conviction and a stronger thesis than what you hold?
2. Is its price action showing underlying strength (outperforming on low timeframe)?
3. Is there a fundamental catalyst (upcoming catalyst, narrative shift)?

## Risk Management That Actually Works

1. Wait for **asymmetric upside** before entering (limited downside, large potential upside)
2. Monitor whether the **thesis remains valid** (not whether price is going up or down)
3. Sell when thesis plays out, is invalidated, or clearly better opportunity exists
4. "Cut your losers early and let your winners run" — make wrong bets small, right bets large
5. Do not add more capital to the market as the bull run progresses

## The Key Principle

"The big money is not made in the individual fluctuations but in the main movement." Find the main move, size into it, and do nothing until the thesis is complete.
