# Fundamentals - Age of project

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis

## Key Takeaways

- Avoiding old "dinosaur coins" (Cardano, Litecoin, Ripple, Decentraland, Curve, etc.) is one of the most important rules for maximizing bull market returns — these coins tend to underperform badly relative to Bitcoin and Ethereum
- The life cycle of a coin: at launch the team is motivated, community is excited, promises are fresh — but after 12 months team tokens unlock, VCs sell, community disappointment grows, and the coin fades into a pool of bag holders waiting to exit
- New coins have more demand and less supply: no bag holders waiting to break even, no insider unlocks selling overhead, and all holders are near or in profit which creates a different holding mentality
- You should own two types of coins: (1) new coins — under 12 months old, haven't had a full bull cycle, in a fresh narrative sector; or (2) multi-cycle outperformers — coins people want to denominate their portfolio in, not just hold temporarily to accumulate something else
- The only confirmed multi-cycle outperformers are Bitcoin, Ethereum, and BNB; Solana is a strong candidate because people began denominating their profits in SOL — treating it as a "stack more of this" asset rather than a stepping stone
- Example of "new beats fundamentals": Jito was valued higher than Lido despite having 35x less revenue and 40x less TVL — because it was new, in a hot narrative, with no bag holders
- If your portfolio looks the same bull market to bull market, you are either a maximalist or making a mistake — portfolios should be completely reorganized each cycle to chase new narratives and fresh projects
