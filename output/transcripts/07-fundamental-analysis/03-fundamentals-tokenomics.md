## Key Takeaways

- Tokenomics is primarily a tool for determining holding period and exit timing — knowing when VC unlocks occur, when inflation kicks in, and when emissions are highest tells you when to sell, not just whether to buy.
- Two main coin types: retail coins (fair launches with no VCs, where retail buys at the same price as insiders) and VC coins (where VCs got in at 10-100x lower prices and treat retail as their exit liquidity at 6-18 month unlock).
- Fair launch coins are preferable because they align incentives — the earliest holders who paid market price are most likely to hold, promote, and create a genuine community rather than dump at the first opportunity.
- VC coins follow a predictable 12-18 month pump-and-dump cycle: VCs get tokens at a steep discount, wait for the unlock, then sell aggressively into retail demand — understanding this is essential to timing exits.
- Tokenomics alone is not sufficient to buy or avoid a coin — it's one box to tick among many, but bad tokenomics (massive upcoming unlocks, heavy VC ownership) can ruin otherwise good investments.
- Good tokenomics setup example: a coin where users must lock up tokens to receive ecosystem airdrops (like TIA) creates constant demand from holders who want the airdrop rewards, reducing sell pressure and creating positive feedback loops.
