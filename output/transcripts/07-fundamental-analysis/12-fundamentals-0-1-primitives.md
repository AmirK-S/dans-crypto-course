# Fundamentals - 0—> 1 Primitives

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis

## Key Takeaways

- A zero-to-one primitive is the first good iteration of a completely new technology or sector in crypto — Bitcoin (first digital cash), Ethereum (first smart contract platform), Unibot (first Telegram trading bot), Rollbit (first casino coin with multiple products) — these consistently deliver the largest returns.
- The first primitive captures the largest return; each subsequent iteration captures less — Bonk went 100x as the first Solana meme coin, WIF went 10x as the second, Silly Dragon went less — the euthanasia rollercoaster means copycats increasingly underperform the original.
- Moats don't exist in crypto — OpenSea lost 80% of its market share to Blur in weeks because there's no switching cost; this is why you must always stay at the bleeding edge rather than betting on incumbents protecting their position.
- "If you tell everyone there's this new primitive no one knows about and they ignore you and think you're an idiot, you're in a great place" — the optimal entry is when the idea is completely unknown; by the time everyone agrees it's the next big thing, the gains have already happened.
- Every 6-12 months a new sector takes over as the dominant trend: DeFi → NFT exchanges → Layer 1s → Telegram bots → casino coins → meme coins → parallelized EVMs → real-world assets — you must position in the next sector before it becomes the consensus.
- The best way to outperform is to find projects in sectors where you need to explain what the sector even does — if the explanation is met with confusion or dismissal, the narrative hasn't been priced in yet and the asymmetric opportunity is still available.
