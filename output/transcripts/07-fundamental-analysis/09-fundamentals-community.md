## Key Takeaways

- A coin's community members ARE its affiliate marketers — every holder who believes in the project is either spreading the word and bringing in new buyers (useful holder) or just waiting to dump (dead weight), and the ratio between these two determines whether a coin can sustain its price.
- The best communities are "indistinguishable from cults" — they have die-hard holders who never want to sell, they make new members feel like outsiders for selling, and they continuously attract developers and new buyers through organic passion.
- HEX and Solana are two case studies: HEX weaponized hate through long-form content to convert critics into buyers; Solana weaponized hate by having an actually great product that converted every skeptic who tried it into an advocate.
- The quality of community members degrades as a coin grows — the early small community is the strongest, most passionate, and most culturally cohesive; flooding the server with thousands of tourists destroys the vibe that attracted the early community.
- Making holders rich is the most powerful community-building tool — a community where everyone is in profit has a die-hard affiliate base that markets the coin for free; a community where VCs dumped and holders are down 90% has no advocates left.
- Key signals of a strong community: cult-like profile picture adoption, unique slang and lore that outsiders don't understand, thriving Discord with ongoing voice calls and active art/meme creation, poaching of top developer talent from other successful projects.
