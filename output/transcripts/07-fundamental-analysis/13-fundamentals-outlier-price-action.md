# Fundamentals - Outlier Price Action

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis
**Loom ID:** 5a5c298e5046441f97a475e8c4f2572e

## Key Takeaways

- Outlier price action — when a coin goes up more and down less than the rest of the market — is treated as a fundamental characteristic because it reflects underlying project strength, not just technicals
- The best coins ignore Bitcoin's price action and perform on their own terms; coins that merely copy Bitcoin with less upside have no reason to be owned over Bitcoin itself
- Outlier price action on a low time frame is a profitable momentum trading strategy: when the market dumps, identify coins that hold their ground, bid them, and ride them as they outperform when the market recovers
- Casper's 100x performance was attributed to four factors: fair launch with no pre-mine or VC allocations, high-conviction holders who understood the project's value vs competitors, slow organic community growth, and launching at the bottom of the bear market
- If a coin is going up while Bitcoin crashes, the supply/demand dynamic is very favorable — less sell pressure, more buy pressure — and that momentum tends to continue
- A practical heuristic: find coins on CoinGecko whose chart looks completely unlike any other coin; if you cannot tell when Bitcoin made its lows from looking at the chart, that is a strong positive signal
- When bad news hits the market and a coin's price does not drop (or rises), it signals that peak fear has passed and an underlying bid exists — this also marks potential market bottoms
