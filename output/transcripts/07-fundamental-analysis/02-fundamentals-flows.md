## Key Takeaways

- Flows is the single most important fundamental analysis tool: if you know where the money will flow, you will inevitably profit — all other analysis (sentiment, narratives, on-chain data, team) is ultimately just telling you where the money is going.
- Identify flows first, then pick coins: if you know money will flow to Coinbase then Base, find the best asset positioned to capture that inflow — even a mediocre coin in a sector with massive inflows will outperform a great coin in a sector with weak flows.
- Key flows analysis questions: Who is your exit liquidity and why will they want to buy? What will cause money to flow into this coin or sector? How hard vs. easy is it to buy right now, and will that change?
- The harder a coin is to buy, the more likely it is mispriced — coins listed only on obscure exchanges (like SpaceMesh on safe.trade) have massive potential inflows waiting for easier access, while Binance listings often mark the end of a rally.
- In bear markets, fundamentals and revenue matter (GMX, Rollbit); in bull markets, memes and narrative matter (Shib, Doge, Bonk, Toshi) — know which phase you're in and allocate accordingly.
- Best sectors based on flows: meme coins on new chains, new Layer 1 protocols, and high-beta assets capturing Coinbase user flows — layer ones and meme coins have driven most of the market cap and performance for seven years running.
