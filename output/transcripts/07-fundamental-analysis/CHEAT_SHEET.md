# Fundamental Analysis — Cheat Sheet

## The Core Mental Model

- Every token is a meme token — price is driven **90% by narrative, community, and flows** and 10% by technology
- "Everything in crypto is a social token... all tokens are a proxy for human attention"
- The goal of fundamental analysis: **identify who your exit liquidity is** before you buy
- Before every entry, ask: "Who is going to buy this from me at a higher price, and why?"

## Level 1 vs Level 3 Analysis

**Level 1 (wrong):** Copying the project's own pitch — technology details, partnerships, white paper, team credentials
**Level 3 (right):** Speculating on other people's future speculation — "who will buy from me, and what story will make them buy?"

"Never analyze using what the project sells you to buy. Analyze for who will buy from you at a higher price."

## Entry Signals

### Highest Hit-Rate Setups

1. **Outlier price action** — coin doesn't drop when the market dumps, or rises while BTC makes lower lows. "If Bitcoin dropping 10% can't make a coin go down, what will?"
2. **Bad news + price doesn't care** — bearish news releases and price holds or rises → bad news is priced in
3. **Perception significantly worse than reality** — the market misunderstands the coin's actual position/strength
4. **New coins on small exchanges** — MEXC, ExBitX, KuCoin, SafeTrade new listing tabs for outlier price action
5. **CoinGecko top gainers during market dumps** — screen top 1,000 by 24h performance during a broad selloff
6. **Zero-to-one primitives** — first good iteration of a genuinely new sector (first smart contract chain, first telegram bot, etc.)

## Narrative and Flows

- **Narratives drive price, not adoption** — a compelling story is what convinces people to buy
- Advanced narrative analysis = predicting which stories will form in the minds of future buyers **before** they form
- Flows analysis = most important tool. Understanding where money is going to flow predicts performance better than anything else
- When to sell: "When your once third-order consequences become the market's front-facing reality" — sell when the thesis is fully priced in

## Tokenomics: What Actually Matters

- Tokenomics tells you **when to exit**, not fundamental value
- **VC coins:** VCs view retail as exit liquidity; most sell within 6-18 months of unlock. "The game is just 12-18 month pump and dumps."
- **Fair launch coins:** Retail buys at equivalent prices to VCs; rewards the holders least likely to sell and most likely to promote
- Avoid coins with large upcoming token unlocks
- Revenue, buybacks, and value-accrual mechanisms do not drive price in bull runs

## Coin Age Rules

**Buy new coins (under 12 months, no prior bull cycle):**
- No bag holders at every price level ready to sell
- Community still motivated and conviction high
- Team still working for the upside

**Must avoid — old "dinosaur" coins:**
- Cardano, Ripple, Litecoin, Mana, Curve, Decentraland
- Overhead supply from bag holders at every level
- Declining community and often founders who have already cashed out
- "Old dino coins is one of the most important rules for maximizing returns in bull runs"

**Exception — multi-cycle outperformers:**
- Bitcoin, Ethereum, BNB, possibly Solana
- People want to denominate their portfolios in these; different dynamic than old altcoins

## The 14 Fundamentals Scoring System

Score each potential investment on 14 criteria (10 points each = 140 max). Only share/buy at 90+.

Key criteria include:
- Narrative/niche strength
- Chain selection
- Community strength and type
- Token distribution (fair launch vs VC)
- Outlier price action
- Team quality
- Market cap relative to upside
- Age of project
- Liquidity and accessibility

Scores are **dynamic** — re-score monthly as conditions change.

## The Thesis Framework

- "Translate your emotional feeling of conviction into a thesis so that it can be tracked and invalidated"
- A thesis must have a **clear invalidation criteria** — without it, you will become a bag holder
- "In investing you don't use a stop loss, you use a thesis instead. The invalidation of a thesis is your stop loss."
- Exit when the thesis plays out OR when the thesis is invalidated — not when price goes up or down

## Finding vs Buying — Separate These

- Finding a coin on Twitter = it has already been talked about and likely already pumped
- Wait for **fear and a proper entry** after finding a coin, not the moment of discovery
- "A coin is only good when its perception is worse than its reality"
- "You must separate the finding and the buying of the coin — separate the conjoined twins"

## Key Rules Summary

1. Know who your exit liquidity is before you buy — if you can't identify them, it's probably you
2. Do not buy old coins — the journey is: promise → insiders sell → VCs unlock → community fades → bag holders
3. Do not read white papers ("couldn't imagine a worse use of time")
4. Do not invest in governance tokens ("a governance token doesn't do shit")
5. The harder it is to buy a coin, the more likely it is mispriced
6. Do not hold altcoins through bear markets — even the best drop 80-95%
7. Bull markets favor memes and growth narratives; bear markets favor revenue and real users
