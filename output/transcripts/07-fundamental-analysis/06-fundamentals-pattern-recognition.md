## Key Takeaways

- "Markets don't repeat but they do rhyme" — the same types of coins outperform every bull run (new Layer 1s, chain-native meme coins, bear-market revenue plays) even though the specific coins change each cycle.
- The new shiny Layer 1 pattern repeats every cycle: a high-level team raises VC money, markets "cheaper and faster than Ethereum," token launches, VCs unlock and sell, the coin dies — Solana is the only coin that has broken this pattern by surviving multiple cycles.
- Chain-native meme coin pattern: Doge led to SHIB (Ethereum meme coin), then Bonk (Solana), now every chain has or wants its own meme coin — the pattern creates opportunities to buy new chain meme coins before the narrative catches mainstream attention.
- Bull market favorites: meme coins, growth narratives, what normies love — fundamentals go out the window. Bear market favorites: revenue, real users, safety (GMX, Rollbit) — the transition between these phases is itself a timing signal.
- When a dominant trend hits a ceiling (Bonk peaks), catch-up plays (WIF, Silly Dragon) typically reach one-third to one-tenth of the leader's market cap — missing the first one often means you can still profit from the second and third.
- Community strength is a repeatable pattern signal: Chainlink (autist community) went 100x, Solana (Solana soldiers community) went 40x from early entry — a passionate, cult-like community that doesn't want to sell is one of the most reliable indicators of future outperformance.
