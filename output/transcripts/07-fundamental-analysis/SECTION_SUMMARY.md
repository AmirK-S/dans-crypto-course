## 07-fundamental-analysis — Trading Playbook Summary

### Core Concepts

- **Crypto is fundamentally a social/attention game, not a technology game.** Every token is a meme token. Price is driven at least 90% by narrative, speculation, marketing, community, and flows — and at most 10% by actual technology or "fundamentals" in the traditional sense. "Everything in crypto is a social token... all tokens are a proxy for human attention."

- **The primary goal of fundamental analysis is to identify who your exit liquidity is.** Before entering any position, the key question is: "Who is going to buy this from me at a higher price, and why?" Level 3 fundamental analysis is entirely focused on speculating on other people's speculation — buying something before the market understands it, then selling when the market fully understands it.

- **Flows analysis is the most important fundamental tool.** Understanding where money is going to flow — which chains, sectors, and coins will receive inflows — is the single most predictive factor for price performance. All other tools (sentiment, narratives, on-chain data, team quality) are ultimately just telling you where the money is going to flow.

- **Narratives drive price, not adoption.** A compelling story (narrative) is what convinces people to buy. "The core of making money in crypto is — it's all about a compelling story." Advanced narrative analysis means predicting which stories will form in the minds of future buyers before those stories are widely understood. The point at which a narrative is fully understood and digested by the market is the point at which you must sell.

- **Tokenomics is primarily about the supply side and timing, not fundamental value.** Tokenomics tells you the holding period of an asset and when to exit based on unlock schedules. The two main types of coins are retail coins (fair launches, no VCs) and VC coins. VCs are extraction-focused — they view retail as exit liquidity and tend to sell within 6–18 months of token unlock.

- **New coins outperform old coins.** You want to own coins that are either (1) new — under 12 months old with no prior bull cycle and no bag holders — or (2) multi-cycle outperformers — rare coins like Bitcoin, Ethereum, BNB, and possibly Solana that people want to denominate their portfolios in. Old dinosaur coins (Cardano, Ripple, Litecoin, Mana, Curve, etc.) must be avoided in bull runs because they carry overhead supply and declining community conviction.

- **Second and third order consequence thinking is how you make money.** Analyzing what a fundamental attribute causes, and then what that causes, is what distinguishes profitable analysis from "face value" analysis. "You buy guessing, estimating, and creating a thesis around what you think might happen and selling when the thesis either is invalidated or it plays out."

### Rules

- **"Before I enter a trade I ask myself who's my exit liquidity. If I can't identify a group of people to be my exit liquidity, it's probably me."**

- **"The main utility of Lido is selling it to other people at a higher price."** (Quoted as the ultimate summary of crypto investing — the only utility of most coins is selling to someone else at a higher price.)

- **"Translate your emotional feeling of conviction into a thesis so that it can be tracked but also invalidated."** A thesis must have an invalidation — if you don't have a thesis with a clear invalidation criteria, you will become a bag holder.

- **"In investing you don't use a stop loss, you use a thesis instead. The invalidation of a thesis is your stop loss."**

- **Do not buy a coin without knowing who you are selling to and why they will buy.** "Whenever you buy something you should know super clearly — oh OK this is the very clear customer avatar who I'm going to be selling this coin to."

- **You must separate finding a coin from buying a coin.** "A coin is only good when its perception is worse than its reality." Finding a coin on Twitter because it is being discussed means it has likely already pumped — wait for fear and the right entry, not the moment of discovery.

- **"If you tell everyone there's this new primitive no one knows about and they ignore you and think you're an idiot, you're in a great place."** Buy what is unknown, sell when it is known.

- **Do not invest in old coins.** "Avoiding old dino coins is one of the most important rules for maximizing your returns in bull runs." The journey of a new project to an old one is: launch with big promises → team gets rich and loses motivation → VCs get unlocked and sell → community fades → bag holders remain praying for a bull run exit.

- **Fair launch coins are preferable to VC coins** because they let retail investors buy at prices equivalent to what VCs would get in private rounds, and they reward holders who are least likely to sell and most likely to promote.

- **Reward the right stakeholders in initial distribution.** "The goal is to make your best holders rich so that they become die-hard soul-locked fans." Rewarding airdrop farmers or bad VCs with a poor "community building score" sets a project up for failure.

- **Bull markets favor memes and growth narratives; bear markets favor revenue and real users.** In bull markets "fundamentals go out the window... it's all about the growth narrative, it's all about buying what normies love." In bear markets, "people are smarter, they're playing things more carefully" and favor coins with real revenue.

- **"You need to sell when your once third order consequences become the market's front-facing reality."** Once the thesis has fully played out and the market understands what you predicted, exit and find the next thing.

- **The harder it is to buy a coin, the more likely it is mispriced.** "The harder it is to buy, the more likely the coin is mispriced as it becomes easier to buy over time."

- **Do not hold altcoins long-term.** "99% of altcoins actually go up less than bitcoin... you can't really hold altcoins for the long term." The only proven long-term holds are Bitcoin, and possibly Ethereum.

- **Never analyze using what the project sells you to buy; analyze for who will buy from you at a higher price.** "There are two different types of analysis: the pitch they're giving you to buy, and the pitch for people buying after you."

### Entry Signals

- **Outlier price action** — A coin that does not go down when the rest of the market dumps, or goes up while bitcoin makes lower lows, is showing underlying strength. "If bitcoin dropping 10% in a day can't make a coin go down, what will?" This is described as "one of the highest hit-rate setups in crypto."

- **Bad news + price not going down** — When bearish news comes out and the price doesn't care, it signals that bad news is priced in and underlying strength exists. "When the market looks weakest, when the news is at its worst, that's the time to buy."

- **Perception significantly worse than reality** — Buy when the perceived value of a coin is significantly lower than what you assess the reality to be. Instructor doubled his Solana position at $19 because he assessed it as severely mispriced relative to its developer ecosystem and community strength.

- **New coins on small exchanges (MEXC, ExBitX, KuCoin, SafeTrade)** — New layer 1 and AI coins get listed on smaller exchanges first. Screening new listings on these exchanges for outlier price action in their early months is one of the two main strategies taught for finding coins before they 10–100x.

- **CoinGecko top gainers during market dumps** — During a broad market selloff, screening the top gainers in the top 1,000 coins on CoinGecko by 24-hour performance identifies coins with relative strength. Look for coins with charts that don't resemble the rest of the market.

- **Zero to one primitives in their first or second iteration** — Buying the first good iteration of a genuinely new technology or sector in crypto (e.g., first smart contract blockchain, first telegram trading bot, first casino coin) is described as one of the best ways to make money. "If you're in one of the first solid protocols of a new primitive, you're probably going to make a lot of money."

- **Strong community with outlier price action + fair launch + new coin** — The combination of a fair launch, a cult-like community, and a coin under 12 months old with no prior bull cycle is presented as the ideal setup (Casper and Toshi are the primary examples used).

- **Thesis-based accumulation when fear is highest** — "The buying process started when the market enters a period of fear, when it enters a period of the perception being much lower than its actual reality."

### Position Sizing / Risk Management

- **The invalidation of the thesis functions as the stop loss.** "In investing you don't use a stop loss, you use a thesis instead. The invalidation of a thesis is your stop loss." If the thesis is invalidated — not just a price level — you sell.

- **Asymmetric upside is the target.** "When you're looking for asymmetric upside you want this much downside if things go wrong and this much upside if things go right." Instructor references a mathematical example where a 20% win rate with a large upside-to-downside ratio results in a net profit.

- **Sell when your third-order consequences become the market's front-facing reality**, not necessarily at a price target. "Once the narrative is fully understood and digested by the market, exit and find the next one."

- **Sell when your intended exit liquidity (the dumb money / late retail) arrives.** "When is the top? It's not about when is the top — it's about when is the person that I want to sell to here." Selling is triggered by the arrival of the target buyer demographic, not a price.

- **Do not hold altcoins through bear markets.** Even the best coins drop 80–95% in bear markets. "Every coin eventually crashes... you can reduce the downtrend but you can't escape it."

- **Avoid coins with large upcoming token unlocks** as a timing consideration. Buying before a significant VC or team unlock is a risk; the unlock may mark a period of underperformance.

- No explicit percentage position-sizing rules or portfolio allocation percentages were stated by the instructor in these transcripts.

### Key Warnings / What Not To Do

- **Do not do level-1 fundamental analysis** — copying a project's own website copy, talking about tech, partnerships, or road maps. "The analysis is basically just what the coin is selling you... not what sells people to buy after you." The instructor explicitly mocks analyses focused on: technology details, scalability claims, team credentials from the website, white paper reading, and partnerships.

- **Do not buy old coins.** "Cardano, Ripple, Litecoin, Mana, Curve, Decentral Land — these coins are a must, must, must avoid." Old coins have bag holders at every level ready to sell, declining community, and often founders who have already sold to fund their lifestyle.

- **Do not buy VC coins without understanding the extraction game.** "Most VC coins die off after 6–18 months." VCs are "very extraction-based" — their goal is to get unlocked and sell to retail as quickly as possible. "The game is just 12–18 month pump and dumps."

- **Do not buy a coin just because the team is great or the technology is good.** "The developers focus on [the 10% of a coin that is fundamentals]. They are masters of the 10% but they're shit at the 90%." Good tech with no narrative, flows, or community does not perform. MakerDAO is cited as an example: "One of the worst performing crypto assets" despite being considered a technically sound project.

- **Do not base your thesis on what the market already knows.** "They didn't understand that they're nothing but publicly available information that has already been consumed and priced in by the market."

- **Do not confuse finding a coin with buying a coin.** Most people buy when they find a coin — which is when it is already being talked about and has already pumped. "There is no differentiation between the finding and the buying of the coin... you have to separate the conjoined twins."

- **Do not make baseless claims in your analysis.** "Might be primed for a move soon based on what? That is a baseless claim." Every forecast must have a reasoning chain.

- **Do not rely on a coin's revenue, buybacks, or value-accrual mechanisms to drive price in a bull run.** "I never want to hear cope about tokenomics revenue or value accrual as having anything to do with the price action of a token. It's just shiny objects and memes."

- **Do not get sucked into other people's narratives at the top.** "Influencers who create these growth narratives... the narrative they explain is the liquidity for the pump that they exit into." Extremely bullish narratives (e.g., "this coin is going to $10,000") are what you sell into, not buy into.

- **Do not hold coins where the community has been destroyed by a 90–95% price decline without a demonstrably exceptional community.** "When you f*** the community by dumping the token 90%, you've lost your affiliates... no one wants it anymore."

- **Do not invest in governance tokens.** "A governance token doesn't do shit... there's no value behind a governance token. Usually you don't want to invest in these."

- **Do not spend time reading white papers.** "White papers — oh, couldn't imagine a worse use of time than reading white papers."
