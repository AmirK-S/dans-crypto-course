# Levels to Fundamental Analysis

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis
**Loom ID:** a491f1a1fb4648f2ad6c7e9cc36e49ce

## Key Takeaways

- Level 1 analysis (beginner): copying influencers, regurgitating what the project's own website says, focusing on partnerships and technology — this is what the project sells you to convince you to buy, not what convinces people to buy from you later at a higher price.
- Level 2 analysis (intermediate): reading research reports, understanding crypto is partly a scam, but still outsourcing thinking to others' opinions and lacking advanced narrative analysis and pattern recognition.
- Level 3 analysis (advanced): the only question that matters is "who is my exit liquidity?" — speculating on others' speculation, building theses the market hasn't priced in yet, treating every coin as a social token driven 90% by narrative and 10% by tech.
- Crypto is fundamentally a game of sales and marketing — 90% of price performance comes from narrative, community, attention, and "pumpermentals" (tokenomics, VCs, chain ecosystem) while tech and fundamentals drive only 10%.
- "Every coin is a meme coin" — Bitcoin is a hard money meme, Ethereum is a banking-the-unbanked meme, Solana is a fast-cheap-easy meme, even traditional stocks are memes for different audience archetypes; embrace this to profit from it.
- The best thesis format: identify second and third order consequences (if Base gets all Coinbase users, they buy the meme coin, which creates a positive feedback loop), build it independently before the market understands it, and know exactly what would invalidate the thesis.
