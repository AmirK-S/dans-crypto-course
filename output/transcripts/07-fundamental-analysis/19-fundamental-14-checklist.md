# 'Fundamental 14' Checklist

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis

## Key Takeaways

- The Fundamental 14 Checklist is a scoring document with 14 fundamental dimensions, each scored out of 10, for a maximum total of 140 points — it is designed to filter coins and prioritize research on the most promising ones
- Fundamental analysis is inherently subjective — the questions are open-ended because there are no hard factual thresholds; analysts must use judgment and conviction rather than formulaic yes/no answers
- The checklist covers all 14 fundamentals taught in the course: flows, tokenomics, advanced narrative, thesis building, pattern recognition, second/third order consequences, team, community, mindshare, perception vs reality, zero-to-one primitives, outlier price action, initial distribution, and age of project
- A practical scoring guideline: coins scoring above 90/140 are worth sharing with the group and looking into further; coins scoring below 90 are generally not worth pursuing unless there is a specific reason to believe the score understates quality
- Scores are dynamic — when an analyst re-scored Toshi after the price rose 50x, the perception vs reality section scored lower because the market had begun catching up to the thesis, which is expected and intentional
- Case study: Toshi was analyzed at a $115 million market cap with a near-perfect score because it was a fair launch, retail-only distribution, Coinbase ecosystem flows, and a strong meme narrative with the market still underpricing future interest
- The checklist output should be shared in the research group for review; coins with very high scores (e.g., 120+) will receive direct feedback on whether to buy
