# Fundamentals - Initial Distribution

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis
**Loom ID:** 9c272599187c4db88e15bd26d8722a52

## Key Takeaways

- Initial distribution — how tokens are first given out when a project launches — is one of the most important fundamentals because it determines the quality of your holder base and shapes the entire lifespan of the project
- Holders are a project's main affiliates and marketers; rewarding people with a high "community building score" (those most likely to promote and least likely to dump) sets the project up for strong organic growth
- Fair launches (like Bitcoin through mining, Yearn through liquidity mining) reward the most valuable early contributors, while ICOs, IEOs, and VC-heavy distributions tend to reward people who immediately dump their allocation
- Airdrop farming case study: Blur went down 90% during an uptrending market because whales who farmed the airdrop at high cost immediately sold to recover expenses, removing all buy pressure
- When you do a fair launch you allow public investors to access the coin at prices that VCs would normally capture exclusively through private rounds — this is why fair-launch coins can go up so much from open market price
- A strong initial distribution creates the foundation of a pyramid — the wider and more loyal the base, the higher the coin can eventually go; a weak distribution (wrong people rewarded) limits how high the pyramid can be built
- The goal is to make the right people rich: loyal, passionate community members who will hold through volatility and bring in new buyers, not pre-salers looking for a quick flip
