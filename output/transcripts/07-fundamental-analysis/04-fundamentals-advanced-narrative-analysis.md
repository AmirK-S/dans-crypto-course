## Key Takeaways

- The core of making money in crypto is a compelling story — people don't say "Bitcoin had a record month of revenue," they say "the government is printing money and you need Bitcoin as a hedge" — narrative drives buying decisions, not metrics.
- Advanced narrative analysis means predicting which stories will form in future buyers' minds before the market understands them — once a narrative is fully understood and digested by the market, that is the exit point, not the entry.
- The lifecycle of a narrative: unknown (best time to buy) → early adopters notice → crypto Twitter picks it up → mainstream media covers it → retail FOMO (best time to sell) → narrative exhaustion → price collapses.
- "If you tell everyone there's this new primitive no one knows about and they ignore you and think you're an idiot, you're in a great place" — the best narrative entries are those where most people think you're wrong.
- Influencers who create growth narratives are building liquidity for their own exits — an extremely bullish influencer narrative about a coin that has already pumped is a sell signal, not a buy signal.
- You need to sell when your third-order consequences become the market's front-facing reality — once what you predicted is now obvious to everyone, exit and find the next misunderstood narrative.
