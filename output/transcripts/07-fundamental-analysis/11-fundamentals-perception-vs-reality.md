# Fundamentals - Perception vs Reality

**Section:** Fundamental Analysis

## Key Takeaways

- "You make money in crypto by understanding something today that people don't yet understand" — buying is right when the perceived value of a coin is significantly lower than its actual reality, and selling is right when perceived value exceeds reality.
- Finding a coin and buying a coin must be treated as two completely separate processes — most people buy when they find something (which means it's already being talked about and pumped); the correct process is to find it, build a thesis, then wait for fear to buy.
- Solana at $19 was the quintessential perception vs. reality trade: second-highest developer count in crypto, best UX on any chain, fastest-growing ecosystem — but trading at a fraction of its fundamental value because of FTX-related hate and FUD.
- A coin can be specifically undervalued AND the market broadly undervalued at the same time — this double undervaluation (coin going up in USD AND outperforming Bitcoin/ETH) is where the most massive outperformance occurs.
- Even the best coin in the world loses 80-95% in a bear market — this means timing the broader market cycle matters more than picking the right coin in isolation; excellent fundamentals don't protect against bear market correlation.
- "A multi-bagger is nothing but the journey of a stock from a point where no one wants it to a point where everyone wants it" — the entire job is to find coins at the zero-interest stage and sell when everyone is interested.
