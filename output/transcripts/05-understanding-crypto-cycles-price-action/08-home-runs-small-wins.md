# Home Runs > Small Wins

**Section:** Understanding Crypto Cycles + Price Action
**Loom ID:** d55ea765774c4b4aa2b984b85bceb637

## Key Takeaways

- 80-90% of your total profit from a bull run will come from just one to three "home run" positions — the rest are small wins or flat positions.
- A home run requires going in with size, having high conviction, and holding the entire way through the thesis playing out — selling early (e.g., at 2x when it could go 100x) means stopping at first base.
- Between home runs, the goal is capital preservation — minimizing losses during the flat/bad periods so you have maximum capital available when the next home run appears.
- Coins with a cult-like community at a small market cap are the best candidates for home runs — a passionate small sample of believers predicts a passionate large sample when more people discover it.
- Reflexivity is what turns a coin into a home run: price goes up → people perceive it as better → more buy → fundamentals improve → price goes higher — new coins without bag holders have easier reflexivity than old coins.
- The correct approach is to hold your winner (the home run) and cut the losers, not sell the winner to fund a loser.
