# The Big Long’/Position Sizing during Bull

**Section:** Understanding Crypto Cycles + Price Action

## Key Takeaways

- The Big Long is a recurring pattern in crypto where price goes parabolic as retail FOMO creates a positive feedback loop of new money bringing in more new money.
- The correct approach is to build spot positions during accumulation, then go maximum risk-on just before the positive feedback loop kicks in (key breakout and retest of significant level).
- Take risk early in the bull run and reduce risk later — most people do the reverse, increasing position sizes as their portfolio grows toward the top.
- 90% of crypto's price appreciation happens in 10% of the time — you must be positioned before the parabolic phase begins, not after.
- As you get into the final eighth of the bull run, move into stablecoins and reduce all risk — do not carry the leverage you used early into the late stage.
- Increasing position sizing at the end of the bull run because your portfolio has grown is a guaranteed way to lose all gains — size down, not up, as prices go higher.
