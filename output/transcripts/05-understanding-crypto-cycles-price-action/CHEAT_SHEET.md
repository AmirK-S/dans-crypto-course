# Understanding Crypto Cycles and Price Action — Cheat Sheet

## The Four-Year Cycle

- Crypto markets repeat in roughly four-year cycles: bull market followed by bear market
- **Timing** of the phases is more reliable than trying to match exact price fractals from prior cycles
- Markets repeat because humans repeat the same psychological mistakes every cycle
- "You learn the lessons when you least need them, and you forget the lessons when you most need them"

## Reflexivity — The Core of Crypto Price Action

- Crypto is a highly **reflexive** asset: higher prices attract more buyers, which improves perceived fundamentals, which raises prices further
- The feedback loop runs in reverse too: lower prices → fewer buyers → worsening perceived fundamentals → even lower prices
- This reflexivity is more pronounced in crypto than in any other asset class
- Understanding reflexivity explains why crypto moves feel "irrational" — they are self-reinforcing, not fundamental

## The Big Long

- Mid-to-late in a bull run, retail FOMO drives a **parabolic positive feedback loop**
- The majority of gains are compressed into a short window of time
- Key rule: Take on **maximum calculated risk BEFORE** the Big Long begins; reduce risk as it progresses
- Never increase leverage or position sizes late in the bull run — that is how people lose everything

## Sector Rotations

- Crypto no longer sees all sectors pumping simultaneously
- Sectors rotate in and out of outperformance, typically lasting 30-90 days each
- **Mean reversion strategy:** Buy sector during capitulation, sell near peak awareness
- **Momentum strategy:** Buy the breakout, ride the trend, be the "fastest horse" in the sector
- Do not mix these two strategies within the same position

## Portfolio Structure

- Maximum **7 coins** in liquid portfolio at any time
- Recommended split: 2-3 long-term holds (BTC/ETH/SOL type) + 3-5 shorter-cycle altcoin positions
- Only hold a new altcoin for **one bull cycle** — buy low, capture the cycle, sell, compound into long-term holds
- "You build wealth through concentration and keep it through diversification"

## Diversification Warning

- Diversifying across many coins in crypto is **not** risk management — it removes asymmetric upside
- 80-90% of your profits will come from 1-3 positions per cycle (the "kingmaker trades")
- Do not sell winners to chase coins that haven't moved yet
- Spreading into 20+ coins after taking partial profits leads to standardized market returns

## Influencer Hierarchy (Information Flow)

```
Original researcher (most alpha)
     ↓
High-level private chats
     ↓
Paid groups
     ↓
Social media / Twitter (you are exit liquidity here)
     ↓
YouTube / mainstream (you are exit liquidity here)
```

- By the time a coin is being shilled on Twitter/YouTube, early participants are selling to you
- "If you bought because of a Tweet or a YouTube vid, you're likely too late or buying near the end"

## Funding Rate as Sentiment Tool

- **Positive funding** (longs paying shorts) = crowd is overly bullish → historically aligns with tops
- **Negative funding** (shorts paying longs) = crowd is overly bearish → historically aligns with bottoms
- Funding rate cannot be manipulated and does not suffer from alpha decay
- "Trade against the herd of derivatives retail traders: buy when others are fearful, sell when others are greedy"

## Key Cycle Rules

1. Earlier in the bull run → take on more risk
2. Later in the bull run → remove risk, move to stablecoins
3. Never increase position sizing as your portfolio grows late in the bull run
4. Do not buy immediately after finding and liking a coin — wait for fear and asymmetric upside
5. Do not try to replicate exact price fractals from past cycles — only timing and sentiment framework transfers
6. Capital preservation during bear markets is the priority: losing 80% in a bear market means 80% less capital for the next opportunity
