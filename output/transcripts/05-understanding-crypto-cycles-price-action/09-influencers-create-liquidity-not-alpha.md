# Influencers Create Liquidity, Not Alpha

**Section:** Understanding Crypto Cycles + Price Action
**Loom ID:** 45f168c9092f4b78b69bc5c82fe3cbdf

## Key Takeaways

- Influencers create liquidity, not alpha — their role is to pump their own bags and sell to their audience, not to make you rich; they buy first and shill to you later.
- There is a hierarchy: original researcher → inner circle friends → paid groups → Twitter/YouTube → public — if you're getting information from social media you are at the bottom, paying the highest price.
- Most bull tweets and coin promotions happen near the top of a coin's cycle as the influencer gains conviction from rising prices — buying at this point means buying during greed.
- Coins have roughly a 30-60 day shelf life of outperformance — once an influencer has shilled it to their full audience, new buyers run out and the coin dies.
- Influencers almost never tell you when they sell — they tell you the entry but not the exit, which is the most important part of making money.
- The correct response is not to never buy coins mentioned online, but to build your own thesis on a coin and buy it during fear, not when an influencer tweets about it at the top.
