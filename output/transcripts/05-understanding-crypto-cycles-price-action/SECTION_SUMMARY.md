## 05-understanding-crypto-cycles-price-action — Trading Playbook Summary

### Core Concepts

- **The Four Year Cycle:** Crypto markets repeat in roughly four-year cycles consisting of a bull market and a bear market. The timing of how long each phase lasts is more reliable than trying to replicate the exact price action fractals from prior cycles. Market cycles repeat because humans repeat the same psychological mistakes — "you learn the lessons when you least need them, and you forget the lessons when you most need them."
- **The Big Long:** A concept describing the period mid-to-late in a bull run when retail FOMO drives a parabolic positive feedback loop. Price appreciation is exponential in nature in crypto, with the majority of gains compressed into a short period of time. The key is to take on maximum calculated risk *before* this period begins and reduce risk as it progresses, not increase it.
- **Reflexivity:** Bitcoin and crypto are highly reflexive assets — higher prices attract more buyers, which improves perceived fundamentals, which raises prices further. This two-way feedback loop is foundational to crypto price action and more pronounced here than in any other asset class. It also runs in reverse: lower prices create fewer buyers, worsening fundamentals.
- **Diversification Is a Myth in Crypto:** All crypto assets are high-beta assets driven by the same macro variables and liquidity conditions. Diversifying across many coins removes asymmetric upside, dilutes research focus, and makes it harder to get good entries and exits. "You build wealth through concentration and keep it through diversification." The recommended liquid portfolio is a maximum of seven coins.
- **Home Runs Over Small Wins:** In crypto, 80–90% of profits come from one to three positions per cycle. The key is to identify these "kingmaker trades" through deep research and fundamental conviction, size into them heavily, and hold them for the full duration of the thesis — not sell winners early to chase other coins.
- **Influencers Create Liquidity, Not Alpha:** There is a hierarchy of information in crypto (original researcher → high-level private chats → paid groups → social media). By the time a coin is being shilled publicly on Twitter or YouTube, participants near the bottom of that hierarchy are getting the worst entry and the least profit. Influencers tweet about coins to create exit liquidity for themselves and their inner circles, not to provide good entries for followers.
- **Funding Rate as Sentiment Tool:** Bitcoin's short-term price action is driven by derivatives traders. Funding rate represents the collective sentiment of those traders. Positive funding (longs paying shorts) signals the crowd is overly bullish — historically aligning with tops. Negative funding (shorts paying longs) signals the crowd is overly bearish — historically aligning with bottoms. Funding does not suffer from alpha decay; it cannot be manipulated.
- **Sector Rotations:** Crypto no longer sees all sectors pumping simultaneously. Individual sectors rotate in and out of outperformance, typically lasting 30 to 90 days each. Winning the rotation game requires either a momentum/swing trading strategy (buy breakouts, ride the trend) or a mean reversion/long-term strategy (buy sector capitulation and sell near peak awareness).

### Rules

- "You become rich by concentrating on something, and you stay rich by diversifying." Apply concentration when building wealth in crypto.
- "Earlier in the bull run, you take on the risk. Later in the bull run, you take off the risk." Position sizing should decrease — not increase — as the bull run matures.
- Maximum of seven coins in a liquid portfolio at any time (excluding locked pre-sales), structured as two to three long-term holds plus three to five shorter-cycle altcoin positions.
- Only hold a new altcoin for a maximum of one bull cycle. "Buy a low, new altcoin, capture its first bull cycle, and then sell it to compound your long-term holds."
- Never increase position sizing simply because your portfolio has grown. "If your position sizing increases as you profit later in the bull, you're doomed to lose all your money when the market hits its top."
- Do not buy a coin immediately after finding it and liking it. "Most people buy a coin after finding it and determining it is good without any consideration for if the upside is asymmetric from the point of entry."
- Do not mix strategies. Long-term investors must buy during fear and sell during greed. If you enter a sector late via momentum but hold as a long-term investor, you will lose money when the coin reverts.
- Avoid following Twitter or YouTube influencer coin calls as a primary investment strategy. "If you bought because a Tweet or a YouTube vid, you're likely too late or buying near the end."
- Do not buy a coin when it is being bull-tweeted at all-time highs with high conviction across social media. "If you're a long-term investor, you can never buy a coin when it's being bull-tweeted like this because you are buying during greed."
- Trade against the herd of derivatives retail traders: "buy when others are fearful and sell when others are greedy."
- "The trend of the long-term is up" for Bitcoin and crypto; short-term price action is driven by derivatives traders and their liquidation dynamics.

### Entry Signals

- **For the Big Long (leverage or heavy allocation):** Enter after a key breakout from an accumulation region, then wait for a retest of a significant level. The instructor describes this as "dark green" — the zone where you build the big long position. This only applies early in the bull run, not late.
- **For long-term asymmetric entries:** Buy when there is "fear amongst the holders and when crypto Twitter has forgotten about the coin." The coin should represent a non-consensus bet with minimal social media discussion at time of entry.
- **Using funding rate:** Negative funding (crowd is overly short/bearish) historically coincides with market bottoms and is a signal to consider buying. Positive funding (crowd is overly long/bullish) historically coincides with tops and is a signal to consider selling or reducing exposure.
- **For sector rotation — mean reversion strategy:** Buy a sector during overly bearish sentiment with identifiable future positive catalysts. Look for a gap between market perception (negative) and underlying reality (strong fundamentals or growing ecosystem).
- **For sector rotation — momentum strategy:** Identify a trending sector early or midway through. Enter on the breakout or on pullbacks/retests after the breakout. Favor the "fastest horse" — the coin that outperforms when the broader market moves up.
- **Bear market top identification:** After the top, when price bounces to the 0.5–0.618 Fibonacci retracement level following the initial crash, and then breaks below prior lows (right shoulder rejection), the bear market is confirmed.

### Position Sizing / Risk Management

- Maximum of seven liquid positions at any time. Recommended structure: two to three long-term multi-cycle holds (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana) plus three to five shorter-cycle altcoin positions.
- "Not too little that you have a high risk of ruin, but not too many that you can't get good entries and exits." Three to five altcoin positions is the stated target range.
- Add to your winners, cut your losers. Do not sell winning positions to chase coins that have not yet moved.
- Reduce leverage and overall risk exposure as the bull run matures. During the final phase of the bull run, move predominantly into stablecoins. Do not hold any leverage when the right shoulder is being rejected after the top is in.
- For the asymmetric upside process: research a coin, build conviction, accumulate the core position during fear, then hold without adding until either the thesis fails (cut position) or the thesis plays out (sell at thesis completion).
- Capital preservation during bear markets and between sector rotations is explicitly stated as the priority: "when the market is in a bear market, the key is to preserve your capital." Losing 80% during a bear market means having 80% less capital to deploy in the next opportunity.
- Leverage trading is described as having a "99.9% failure rate" and should be avoided by the vast majority of participants. The only narrow exception the instructor acknowledges is using leverage very early in a confirmed Big Long — and only for those already operating at a top-1% analyst level.

### Key Warnings / What Not To Do

- Do not hold 25 coins (or any highly diversified portfolio) in crypto. "Diversification is $10,000 into 50 coins. You're an idiot." It removes asymmetric upside, dilutes research focus, and makes it harder to take profit at the end of the bull run.
- Do not increase your position sizing as your portfolio grows later in the bull run. This is described as one of the primary ways people "lose all their money when the market hits its top."
- Do not use leverage trading unless you are a top-1% market analyst who can identify the Big Long early. "Leverage trading is a pursuit that has a 99.9% failure rate."
- Do not become a trader late in the bull run. "The biggest reason why people lose money in crypto, one of the biggest, is they become traders late in the bull run." This is described as a market cycle symptom — chasing pumps with leverage and no risk management.
- Do not buy coins because they are being heavily shilled on social media or Twitter at all-time highs. "If you long-term invest a Twitter shill coin... you must wait to buy fear." Coins shilled at highs have asymmetric downside, not upside.
- Do not copy influencer calls without building your own thesis and waiting for a fearful entry point. Influencers will not tell you when they sell. "They tell you where to buy but that doesn't matter, it's about selling."
- Do not try to use past cycle price fractals to predict exact future price action. "You can't use the exact specifics of the past cycles to determine the next ones" — only the timing and sentiment framework transfers.
- Do not sell a winning home run position early to chase a coin that has not yet moved. "You sold the winning position at a 2x, now the thing's up 100x."
- Do not chase the flavor-of-the-month sector if you are a long-term investor. "Biggest mistake is you think the flavor of the month coins on Twitter are the best holds going forward."
- Do not buy when it feels safe — "for most, a safe entry is already too late and leads to big losses." Buying when the crowd is comfortable means buying during greed, not fear.
