# 4 Year Cycle

**Section:** Understanding Crypto Cycles + Price Action
**Loom ID:** b702be085b6b43a48e670ba0b5dd31b8

## Key Takeaways

- The four-year cycle represents the repeating bull and bear market cycle in Bitcoin, where from the exact bottom to the exact top has historically been the same number of days across cycles.
- The primary driver of the four-year cycle is market psychology: people learn the lesson of taking profit at the bottom, but forget it by the time the next top arrives three to four years later.
- You can use the four-year cycle to understand timing (how long bull markets last, when to expect tops) but cannot use exact past price fractals to predict future price action — the market punishes those who try.
- A Bitcoin ETF could potentially break or alter the four-year cycle by introducing a consistent passive bid from the traditional finance system, similar to how stocks perpetually drift higher.
- The election cycle (every four years) correlates with crypto cycles because governments want a healthy economy going into elections.
- "In market cycles, you learn the lessons when you least need them, and you forget the lessons when you most need them" — this explains why the same mistakes repeat every cycle.
