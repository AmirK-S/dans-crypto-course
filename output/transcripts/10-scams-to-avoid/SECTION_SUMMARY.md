## 10-scams-to-avoid — Trading Playbook Summary

### Core Concepts

- There are three main passive income methods in crypto: staking, yield farming, and liquidity providing — all involve depositing money to do a task in exchange for yield.
- "Real yield" is a sustainable yield paid from actual usage of the platform or network (e.g., trading fees, transaction fees, borrower interest, options premiums, insurance premiums). "Fake yield" is yield paid from token inflation — it is unsustainable and does not represent real earnings.
- Fake yield inflates apparent returns (e.g., 1500% APY) by printing new tokens, but since more tokens are in circulation the token value drops proportionally — leaving the holder no better off.
- Prop firms (funded trading accounts) are described as misleading and "a bit shady": they charge fees for access to a challenge/evaluation, most traders never pass and lose their fee, and many firms do not actually open real trades — they simulate positions and profit from the fees of traders who fail.
- Leverage is described as a tool for trading with less money, not more. Using leverage increases emotions, stress, and mistakes, making it harder to be profitable — especially for new traders.
- Prop firms monetize through: (1) fees from traders who never pass the evaluation, (2) selling customer data to trading firms who use it to target stop-losses, and (3) counter-trading poor performers.
- The framework for identifying a scam: pull back the layers to understand the actual business model and ask whether the incentives of the provider are aligned with the trader making money.

### Rules

- "If the yield is high, always consider what underlying risks are associated."
- "Passive income in general, I recommend staying away from in crypto. It's not asymmetric in nature."
- Distinguish real yield from fake yield before deploying capital into any yield-generating opportunity.
- "Avoid prop firms and funded trading accounts. Focus on the strategies that I teach in this mastermind."
- "If you want to make money from trading, you probably should learn from people who benefit from you being profitable."
- Leverage "allows you to trade with less money" — that is the correct mindset. Using leverage to trade with more money than you otherwise would is the wrong approach.

### Position Sizing / Risk Management

- The instructor frames desirable opportunities in terms of asymmetric risk/reward: "you want to be making 10 reward for every [1 risk]" as a general principle for investing in crypto.
- Yield farming is characterized as having a poor risk/reward profile: "you potentially lose 100% if something goes wrong and make what, 20%, 50% — so your risk to reward is 0.5 risk for one reward... that's a bad ratio."
- Buying Bitcoin near bear market lows is given as an example of asymmetric opportunity: "your downside is limited, but your upside equals massive, maybe 5x potentially."

### Key Warnings / What Not To Do

- Do not chase high-yield farming opportunities advertising extreme APYs (e.g., 1500% per year) — these are fake yield scams.
- Do not confuse token inflation-based returns with real income; receiving more tokens that are worth proportionally less is not making money.
- Do not use leverage with the goal of trading a larger position than you could otherwise afford — that is the wrong mindset and leads to more emotional, less profitable trading.
- Do not pay fees to prop firms/funded account programs: "you pay money to these f****** and then they don't even give you all the profit" and most traders "pay the fee to pass the test and never pass."
- Do not trust prop firms for trading education: "the prop firm doesn't want you all to make money because if you all make money, their business model dies. So they're never gonna train you with what you need to make money."
- "AI does not make you money. AI does not suddenly make your bot make money" — treat AI trading bot claims with the same skepticism applied to other scams.
- Avoid yield farming broadly: "I would avoid yield farming if I was you. Definitely avoid fake yield farms."
