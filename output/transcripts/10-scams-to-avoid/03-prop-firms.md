# Prop Firms

**Section:** Scams to Avoid
**Loom ID:** 414b1f2eeaf94a35af035a09205fc446

## Key Takeaways

- Prop firms (funded trading accounts) work by charging a non-refundable fee ($50-$900) for an evaluation period — most traders fail the evaluation and the prop firm keeps the fee without ever risking real capital
- A critical hidden truth: most prop firms never actually place the trades — they run simulated/fake trading environments because they know statistically that 90%+ of traders lose, so it's more profitable to not open real positions and just pocket the evaluation fees
- When a trader does "win," the payout comes out of the prop firm's cash reserves, not from real trading profits — this creates a model where large winners can bankrupt smaller prop firms, leaving traders unpaid
- Leverage is fundamentally misunderstood by most prop firm customers: leverage is a tool to use LESS money on an exchange while keeping the rest in safer assets — it is NOT a tool to control more money than you have; this misuse leads to emotional over-trading and guaranteed losses
- Direct comparison: Binance margin trading costs 0.1% per trade with no access fee, no time limits, and you keep 100% of profits — prop firms charge $100-$900 upfront, take 20% of profits, impose time limits, and may not honor payouts
- Prop firms profit through three mechanisms: (1) evaluation fees from traders who never pass; (2) selling customer trade data to market makers who front-run retail; (3) counter-trading bad traders' positions
- The broader lesson: any entity that needs you to fail to sustain its business model will never provide education that actually makes you profitable — learn only from people who benefit from your success
