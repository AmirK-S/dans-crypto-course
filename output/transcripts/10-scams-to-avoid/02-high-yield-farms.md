# High Yield Farms

**Section:** Scams to Avoid
**Loom ID:** b09b1fc9ab8d40528d2535961970a01a

## Key Takeaways

- There are two types of yield: real yield (paid from actual platform revenue like trading fees, transaction fees, borrower interest) and fake yield (paid from token inflation — you get more tokens but each token is worth less, so you haven't actually made money)
- The clearest red flag for fake yield is extremely high APY percentages (hundreds or thousands of percent) — these are always paid through token inflation, which dilutes the value of existing tokens proportionally
- The fake yield cycle: project launches high-yield program → users deposit capital → TVL metrics look great → metrics get promoted → retail speculates on token → token inflates to pay yield → repeat until unsustainable
- Real yield example: staking Ethereum through Lido at ~4% APY, where yield comes from actual network transaction fees paid by users — this is sustainable and not dilutive
- Even real yield is generally not recommended because the risk-reward is asymmetric in the wrong direction: you risk 100% loss (smart contract hack, project failure) for 4-50% annual gain — compare this to buying a coin at a bear market bottom where downside is limited and upside can be 5-10x
- The key investing principle: always prefer asymmetric opportunities — limited downside, massive upside. Yield farming gives you the opposite: unlimited downside, capped upside
- "Bitcoin ETF token" offering 1500% APY is cited as an obvious example of a fake yield scam — anyone offering guaranteed multi-hundred percent returns is paying it from inflation, not revenue
