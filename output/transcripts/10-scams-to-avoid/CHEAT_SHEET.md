# Scams to Avoid — Cheat Sheet

## The Framework for Identifying a Scam

Ask two questions about any opportunity:
1. What is the actual business model? (Pull back all the layers)
2. Are the incentives of the provider aligned with you making money?

If the provider profits when you fail — it's a scam.

## Fake Yield vs Real Yield

| | Real Yield | Fake Yield |
|-|-----------|------------|
| Source | Actual platform usage — trading fees, borrower interest, transaction fees | Token inflation — new tokens printed and distributed as "yield" |
| Sustainability | Sustainable as long as the platform has users | Unsustainable — dilutes token value proportionally |
| Example | Lido (staking rewards from Ethereum validator fees) | Yield farm advertising 1,500% APY |
| Net result | You earn money | You receive more tokens worth proportionally less = net zero |

**Rule:** "If the yield is high, always consider what underlying risks are associated."

**The APY test:** 1,500% APY is almost certainly fake yield. Ask yourself: where does this money come from? If the answer is "new token emissions," it is fake.

## High-Yield Yield Farming — Avoid

- The risk/reward is fundamentally broken: "you potentially lose 100% if something goes wrong and make 20-50% — that's 0.5 reward for 1 risk"
- Contrast with asymmetric opportunity: Bitcoin near bear market lows has limited downside and 5x+ upside
- "Passive income in general — I recommend staying away from in crypto. It's not asymmetric in nature."
- **Exception:** Real yield from established protocols (Lido, Aave, dYdX fees) is legitimate — but still not the primary strategy; "we're here for delta"

## Prop Firms / Funded Trading Accounts — Avoid

**How prop firms actually make money (not from trading profits):**
1. Fees from traders who pay the evaluation fee but never pass
2. Selling customer trading data to firms that use it to target stop losses
3. Counter-trading identified losing traders

**Why they don't want you to succeed:**
- "If you all make money, their business model dies"
- They will never train you with what you actually need to be profitable
- Most traders "pay the fee to pass the test and never pass"
- Many firms do not even open real trades — they simulate positions

**The leverage misunderstanding:**
- Leverage = a tool to trade with **less money**, not more
- Using leverage to trade more than you can afford increases emotions, stress, and mistakes
- More emotions = less profitability, especially for new traders

## General Passive Income Warning

The recommendation for most participants: **avoid passive income strategies in crypto entirely.**

Focus on the asymmetric strategies instead:
- Long-term investing (buy low during fear, sell during greed)
- Presales (bear market entry, bull run exit)
- Airdrops (free tokens from protocol participation)

These have asymmetric risk/reward. Yield farming and staking do not.

## Red Flags Checklist

- APY above ~15-20% from a source you can't explain
- Requires depositing funds to participate in "yield"
- Claims to have an algorithm that "always makes money"
- "AI does not make you money. AI does not suddenly make your bot make money"
- Prop firm promising to pay you with their capital after a fee
- Commission rates above 20% in any sales/affiliate program
- "Guaranteed job placement" in exchange for $5,000-$10,000 upfront
