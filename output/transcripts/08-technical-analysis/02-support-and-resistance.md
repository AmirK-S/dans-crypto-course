# Support and Resistance

**Section:** Technical Analysis
**Loom ID:** 18ea977f35864d1594ea0f0867e2dee5

## Key Takeaways

- Support and resistance is used for four things: identifying breakout points, finding buy zones, understanding trend strength — not typically for setting sell targets on bull-market altcoins (use thesis invalidation instead)
- Draw support/resistance lines only at meaningful levels — breakouts from downtrends and horizontal price levels where candles close repeatedly; ignore trivial lines that most beginners over-draw
- Only use the 4-hour and daily time frames for drawing levels; candle close (not wick extremes) is the best level to draw from
- The strength of a support level diminishes with each successive test — think of buyers at a level as a tank of water that drains with each touch; when the tank empties, the level breaks
- A key insight: the harder it is to buy a dip, the stronger the trend — a coin that holds support for only 2-3 days and then rockets is a much stronger signal than one that chops sideways for months before failing
- For meme coins and altcoins going to new all-time highs, resistance levels become irrelevant for selling — use thesis completion as the exit signal rather than price chart resistance
- The breakout-and-retest pattern (price breaks above resistance, pulls back to that level as new support, then moves up) is a high-quality entry setup on strong newer coins
