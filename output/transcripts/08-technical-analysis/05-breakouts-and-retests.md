# Breakouts and Retests

**Section:** Technical Analysis
**Loom ID:** 271661cb466b4de486606a3c4624367a

## Key Takeaways

- The core breakout-and-retest pattern: price forms an accumulation range after a decline, breaks out above the range on high volume, pulls back to retest the breakout level as new support, then moves up — this is the primary entry signal used for smaller coins and meme coins
- High volume on the breakout is the key confirmation — a "middle finger" volume spike on the breakout candle indicates strong demand; a low-volume breakout is weak and untrustworthy
- This pattern directly maps to the broader market cycle: accumulation phase → breakout → bull trend; conversely, distribution phase → breakdown → bear trend; buying breakouts and selling breakdowns can time both entries and exits
- A retest does not have to return all the way to the breakout level — any meaningful pullback after a high-volume breakout in which volume then declines can be an entry point
- This strategy works best on meme coins and smaller altcoins with organic price action; Bitcoin's price action is too complex and data-driven to rely on simple breakout-and-retest patterns
- Common investor mistake: buying 20% dips on a coin that does 60% pullbacks, getting bored during the extended accumulation range, selling to chase another coin — then missing the eventual breakout; patience during accumulation is what the pattern rewards
- The strategy is not used in isolation — it is one tool in a toolkit alongside fundamental analysis, retracement measurements, and volume analysis
