# Retracements and Rallies

**Section:** Technical Analysis
**Loom ID:** 87b763fb75e44b268c9bddcdb6548a98

## Key Takeaways

- Setting rough guidelines for expected retracements and rallies before you buy or sell dramatically reduces FOMO, increases patience, and leads to better entries and exits
- Typical bull market retracements by asset class: Bitcoin ~20%, Ethereum ~20-25%, Solana ~30-50%, meme coins ~40-60%; use these as buy-the-dip targets rather than buying after any minor pullback
- Typical bear market retracements: Bitcoin ~77-80%, Ethereum ~80%, Solana ~95% (FTX-affected), meme coins ~93-98% — wait for 80-90% decline across altcoins before aggressively accumulating in a bear market
- Expected bull run rally multiples from cycle bottom: Bitcoin 4-8x, Ethereum similar, Solana 15-30x, meme coins 10-100x — when a coin has already done its "usual pump," consider reducing risk rather than assuming more upside
- The reason smaller coins justify larger position size despite higher risk: they offer asymmetric returns (10-100x) that Bitcoin or Ethereum simply cannot replicate; the risk premium must be reflected in the expected return
- Markets rhyme, they don't repeat exactly — use historical retracement and rally percentages as rough guides, not precise predictions
