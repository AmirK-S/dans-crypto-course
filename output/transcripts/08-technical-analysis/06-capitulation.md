# Capitulation

**Section:** Technical Analysis
**Loom ID:** a1ed9b51048446a3b146848aa9a0564c

## Key Takeaways

- Capitulation is the point of maximum fear and forced selling, where price trades far below fair value — and it represents the best buying opportunity in crypto because sellers are willing to sell at the lowest prices they would ever consider
- Cascading liquidations occur when a price drop liquidates leveraged longs, forcing them to sell, which drops the price further and liquidates more longs — this chain reaction creates temporary, violent bottoms ideal for spot buyers
- Forced sellers (bankrupt firms, margin calls, liquidated traders) have no choice about price — they accept whatever the market offers, which is why bottoms often form during forced selling events like the FTX collapse
- A liquidation event transfers wealth from the derivatives trading pool to long-term spot holders — it shrinks the number of coins available for sale, making it easier for price to rise afterward
- Five tools used to measure capitulation: (1) Hunter Algorithm (funding below zero + price below moving average); (2) Realized Losses (negative = people losing big = likely bottom); (3) Long Liquidation data (spike = forced selling floor); (4) Bid/Ask Depth (surge in buy orders at bottom shows smart money stepping in); (5) DEX volume spikes (large volume at price extremes = hands changing at the bottom)
- Capitulation events typically occur 1-3 times per year and represent some of the highest-conviction buying opportunities available
- These tools work best on Bitcoin and top-tier assets; they are less directly applicable to meme coins, though the concept of buying fear and forced selling applies universally
