# Technical Analysis — Cheat Sheet

## Role of Technical Analysis

- TA is **20% of the process** — the other 80% is fundamental research
- Three uses only: (1) spotting entry points, (2) identifying exits, (3) understanding market cycle phase
- "The less you use charts as an investor, I think the better"
- Stronger fundamentals = cleaner TA setups = higher win rates (works best on new strong coins, not old weak ones)

## Support and Resistance

- Draw only **meaningful lines** — not every level has equal value
- Draw at **candle body closes**, not wicks, especially on higher timeframes
- Use only **4-hour and daily** timeframes
- Two types of lines: breakout from downtrend + horizontal support/resistance

**Support Strength Rule:**
- Each additional tap of a support level = sign of weakness (buyers at that level are being depleted)
- First test of support is the strongest
- If a coin spends a long time at support without bouncing → sign of weakness, not strength
- "The harder it is to buy the dip, the stronger the trend"

## Volume Signals

Volume = proxy for attention and interest.

| Price | Volume | Signal |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Up | Up | Bullish |
| Down | Down | Bullish (healthy pullback) |
| Up | Down | Bearish (weak move) |
| Down | Up | Bearish (strong selling) |

- Do not use Binance volume data for large coins — may not be accurate
- Volume is most reliable and useful for smaller meme coins
- "You want to see volume come in on the breakout" — low-volume breakout is not worth buying

## Retracement Guidelines (Bull Market)

| Asset | Typical Bull Market Retracement |
|-------|-------------------------------|
| Bitcoin | ~20% |
| Ethereum | ~25% |
| Solana | ~30-50% |
| Meme coins | ~40-60% (sometimes 70%) |

"Why are you buying a 20% dip on a meme coin? You know the average retracement."

## Expected Rally Ranges (Cycle Bottom to Top)

| Asset | Expected Rally |
|-------|--------------|
| Bitcoin | 4-8x |
| Ethereum | Similar to Bitcoin, slightly more |
| Solana | 15-30x |
| Meme coins | 10-100x |

Use these ranges to calibrate when to start reducing risk, not as price targets.

## Breakout and Retest Pattern

1. Price accumulates at resistance (volume declining)
2. **High-volume breakout** above resistance
3. Price retests the broken resistance as new support
4. **Buy on the retest** — this is the preferred entry

Best suited for: meme coins and small-cap coins. Not reliable on Bitcoin or large-caps.

## Capitulation — The Best Buying Opportunity

Capitulation = forced selling, cascading liquidations, intense fear. "Smart people buy capitulation."

**5 Tools Used Together (Never in Isolation):**
1. Hunter Algorithm — buy signal when negative funding + price below moving average
2. Realized Losses — deeply negative = forced sellers present
3. Long Liquidations — large spike = cascading forced exits
4. Bid/Ask Depth — spike in bid depth = smart money accumulating
5. DEX Volume + Aggregate Funding — confirmation of extreme sentiment

"No one will sell a coin lower than a forced seller" — capitulation = true bottoms.

## Stop Losses for Investors vs Traders

- **Investors:** No percentage stop loss. Exit is determined by **thesis invalidation**, not a price level
- **Traders:** Chart-based stop loss acceptable — risk/reward setup from levels
- "I don't set a 5% stop loss on investments. It's about the thesis. The thesis is what gives you the sell region."
- For meme coins specifically: "I sell when the thesis plays out. I don't give a f*** about resistance."

## What TA Does NOT Work On

- Bitcoin and large-cap coins — basic support/resistance, breakouts, and retests are unreliable. More advanced off-chart data needed (funding rates, on-chain, etc.)
- Old, weak coins like Litecoin — "weak coins produce unreliable technical patterns"
- Do not apply retracement guidelines for one coin type to another (e.g., 20% dip rules for BTC do not apply to meme coins)
