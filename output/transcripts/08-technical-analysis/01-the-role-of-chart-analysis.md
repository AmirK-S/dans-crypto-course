# The Role of Chart Analysis

**Section:** Technical Analysis
**Loom ID:** deb5536034b14557a9679ace1620b025

## Key Takeaways

- Technical analysis is only about 20% of the overall investing process — fundamental research comes first; the chart is used primarily to time entries and exits, not to decide what to buy
- The key use cases for chart analysis are: (1) spotting outlier price action, (2) identifying entry points on breakout/retests or large pullbacks, and (3) understanding which phase of the market cycle a coin is in
- Technical analysis works much better on strong new coins than on weak old coins — Litecoin repeatedly faked out breakout trades because its price action was weak and fundamentally uninspiring
- For large caps like Bitcoin, basic support/resistance is less effective; what works better is using derivative data sets — funding rates, realized losses, bid/ask depth, long liquidation data — to measure sentiment and capitulation events
- The ideal process: find a great coin using the Fundamental 14, then use chart analysis to get a good entry (breakout/retest or 40-60% dip depending on coin type), rather than starting from chart patterns and working backwards
- Do not set percentage-based stop losses as an investor — stop losses should be based on thesis invalidation; if the fundamental thesis still holds, short-term price drops are not a reason to sell
- Two meme coin TA buying strategies: (1) breakout from accumulation range with high volume followed by a retest of support; (2) buying 40-60% pullbacks on strong coins with outlier price action
