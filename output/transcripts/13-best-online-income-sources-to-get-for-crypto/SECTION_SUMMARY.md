## 13-best-online-income-sources-to-get-for-crypto — Trading Playbook Summary

### Core Concepts

- High ticket sales is described as "the fastest and easiest way to make 10k online" and is presented as the primary income source to fund a crypto portfolio. The instructor (Dan) states he used money earned from high ticket sales to enter the crypto bull run and reach his first seven figures.
- High ticket sales involves working as a remote closer or appointment setter for online education/coaching businesses, earning commissions on sales of products priced from $3,000 to $50,000+.
- There are two main entry-level roles: appointment setter (reaching out to warm leads to book calls; income range cited as $3,000–$15,000/month) and closer (taking sales calls and converting prospects; income range cited as $10,000–$50,000+/month).
- The commission structure for closers is typically 10–20% of the deal size. Commission rates above 20% are flagged as a red flag. As an example: 10% of a $5,000 product = $500 per sale.
- Networking — not cold outreach or job boards — is described as the primary and most effective strategy for landing high ticket sales roles. Research cited from LinkedIn shows 70% of people were hired at companies where they already had a connection; for desirable companies the figure rises to 95%.
- Sales at its core is described as asking the right questions to help prospects reach their own conclusions, not pressuring them. The instructor states: "What you say is garbage and what they say is gospel" and "a man convinced against his will is of the same opinion still."
- Combining high ticket sales income with crypto investing is presented as the core two-part path to financial independence: earn consistent online income through sales, then allocate that income into crypto during bull runs.

### Rules

- "High ticket sales is the fastest and easiest way to make 10k online" — presented as a foundational premise of the section.
- "What you say is garbage and what they say is gospel" — the instructor's guiding principle for sales calls.
- "A man convinced against his will is of the same opinion still" — allow the prospect to persuade themselves, not the other way around.
- Your presentation should never be longer than 10% of your total call length.
- Never assume anything about a prospect's situation or goals — always ask clarifying questions to precisely identify their circumstances.
- The goal of networking meetings is not to ask for a job; it is to build relationships, gather information, and generate referrals.
- Do not copy and paste outreach templates — use them as a structural principle, not a script.
- "If you do what other struggling people do, you will struggle" — over-prepare for interviews compared to the average applicant.
- Always ask "How can I help you?" in networking conversations — add value, do not only extract it.
- Do not directly cold-message business owners asking to be hired as a closer; it will not work and signals a lack of understanding of how the industry operates.
- When screening offers, assess whether the company's goals align with yours and whether it can realistically pay you what you want — do not jump at the first offer out of excitement.
- Avoid any program or person that guarantees placement into a job in exchange for $5,000–$10,000: "Guaranteed placement does not guarantee jobs... a lot of beginners end up falling into that trap and losing a lot of money."
- Typical commission rates are 10–20%; anything above 20% is described as likely a scam.
- High ticket sales is defined as products priced at $3,000 and above, more typically $5,000+.

### Position Sizing / Risk Management

- The instructor explicitly states that having a steady online income (such as from high ticket sales) makes managing a crypto portfolio psychologically easier: "Having income makes managing your portfolio easier. If you solely rely on your crypto portfolio for money it's not very fun... if you lose all your money you're back at square one."
- Having consistent income allows a trader to "deal with losing 10k in a day, 20k in a day, because you know that you've got that income."
- No specific guidance on position sizing, risk percentages, or crypto exit criteria is given in this section.

### Key Warnings / What Not To Do

- Do not rely solely on your crypto portfolio for income — it makes emotional management of the portfolio extremely difficult.
- Do not use cold outreach (cold DMs, job boards, or mass resume submissions) as your primary strategy to land high ticket sales roles: "In 2024, the worst way to land a role in this industry is by far going through cold games... it doesn't work."
- Do not pay programs that claim to guarantee job placement: "Guaranteed placement does not guarantee jobs... it's just not the way this industry works."
- Do not jump at the first offer without screening it — assessing the quality and suitability of the offer is described as critical to long-term success.
- Do not make your sales presentation longer than 10% of the total call — this is described as "a big mistake a lot of new salespeople make."
- Do not assume you know what a prospect means without clarifying — "never assume anything" is stated as a cardinal sin in sales.
- Do not try to get hired by directly messaging business owners asking for a closer role: "Christian, do you need closers, part of being direct? No, I do not. And that is a classic example of how you do not get hired."
- Do not think an accent or non-native English is a disqualifier — the instructor explicitly dismisses this concern and cites counterexamples.
- Do not move to a low-tax country after making large crypto gains — do it before, to avoid exit taxes and capital gains taxes on already-realised wealth.
