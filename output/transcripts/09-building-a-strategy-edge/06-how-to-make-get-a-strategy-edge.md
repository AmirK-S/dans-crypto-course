# How to make/get a strategy/edge?

**Section:** Building a Strategy/Edge
**Loom ID:** 232a209f4add4234a775002a26c26ca6

## Key Takeaways

- An edge by definition cannot be replicated by following a step-by-step guide — if it could be, everyone would do it and the edge would disappear; building a real edge requires figuring things out that don't have documented answers
- The five components of building a strategy: (1) Data — what real data backs it, is it back-tested and forward-tested, does it suffer alpha decay; (2) Confluence — multiple data sets confirming the same signal; (3) Intuition — can you distinguish fear from genuine intuition; (4) Network — who helps you build and refine the system; (5) Refinement — constantly updating as markets change
- The three investment styles to choose from: mean reversion (buying extreme fear/bottoms), trend following (ride the momentum, exit when trend reverses), pattern trading (breakout/retest setups) — your personality type often determines which suits you best
- The difference between trading and investing strategies: trading requires rigid, numbers-based, non-discretionary rules with risk management journaling; investing allows more intuition and patience-based execution
- You already have access to the full investing system from this course — what to buy (Fundamental 14), when to buy (technical analysis tools), when to sell (thesis validation), how to minimize taxes, and how to bank — building your own is only needed if you want to go beyond what is taught
- For trading systems: back-test must show the system works across a wide range of input parameters, not just a single precise combination — if changing the moving average period by one breaks the system, the data is fake and coincidental
- If your intuition is underdeveloped (you're a beginner), follow a system exactly without discretion; as your pattern recognition grows over years, intuition will become a more reliable edge than any rigid rule set
