# Follow the pro or become the pro

**Section:** Building a Strategy/Edge
**Loom ID:** d79a5a7eea5f47d588ffd4bb1be3b4b9

## Key Takeaways

- There are exactly two paths to making money in crypto: follow a genuine pro very closely, or become a pro yourself — staying an amateur is not an option if you want results
- Following a pro means getting close enough to get personalized answers, full context, and both entry AND exit signals — Twitter and YouTube only give you the buy side; pros never publicly share when they sell
- A critical mistake: only follow 1-2 pros maximum, follow everything they say without being selective, and never deviate based on your own incomplete analysis — Toshi went 25x and many students who didn't buy it when told missed the entire move
- The higher someone's conviction on a coin, the higher the price likely already is — the coin you should buy when copying a pro is often the one they're least excited about, because that's the one that's still early
- You cannot copy traders profitably: the millisecond timing difference between their entry and your execution compounds into consistent losses; copy investing (not trading) works because entries are patient and time-insensitive
- Becoming the pro takes a minimum of 3-5 years and full market cycle experience — the path is: pay tuition through losses → find mentors → become their number one student → develop your own system → become independent
- The most successful investors always became the #1 student of one specific mentor in their domain — find the best person, get into their highest-level community, learn deeply, then eventually surpass them
