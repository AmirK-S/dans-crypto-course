## Key Takeaways

- 99% of trading algorithms are scams built on back-tested fake data — creators find a combination of arbitrary parameters that looks good in historical data, then sell it without forward-testing in live markets
- Back-testing alone means almost nothing because markets change constantly — the only proof that matters is forward-tested results with live signals that other users actually received and traded on
- Real data vs fake data: real data sources (like funding rates) actually impact how markets move; fake data sources (like moving average crossovers) don't — the fact that a moving average is at a certain level doesn't cause buyers or sellers to act differently
- Funding data is one of the only data sets that does not suffer from alpha decay — even if everyone looks at negative funding simultaneously, the market is still bearish and the signal is still valid; knowing something doesn't change the underlying reality it represents
- The Hunter Algorithm sends signals based on the prior day's closed candle data (not live candle data), so signals never repaint — there is a 15-second window after daily open for data to transmit, after which the signal is finalized
- A well-designed system must work across a range of input parameters, not just one precise setting — if changing a moving average period by one breaks the whole system, the parameters were chosen by coincidence in back-testing, not because they reflect real market forces
- The Hunter Algorithm has been forward-tested with live signals since 2021 and maintains approximately 84% accuracy on buy signals — this is the key differentiator from back-test-only systems
