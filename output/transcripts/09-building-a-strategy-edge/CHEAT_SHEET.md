# Building a Strategy Edge — Cheat Sheet

## The Two Paths (No Middle Ground)

**Path 1: Follow the Pro**
- Find one high-level practitioner and implement their system exactly
- Do EVERYTHING they say — being selective defeats the purpose
- Maximum 1-2 people to follow (conflicting strategies from multiple sources produces net losses)
- "If you follow everything they do, it makes money. Anything you do selectively by default won't make money."

**Path 2: Become the Pro**
- 3-5 years of consistent, dedicated study
- Requires mentors, a network, and a full market cycle of firsthand experience
- You cannot learn trading lessons secondhand — "every mistake must be made from first-hand experience"
- "You either follow the pro or become the pro. Anything outside this leads to losing money."

## Strategy vs Non-Strategy

**Not a strategy:**
- "Buying the dip"
- Following Twitter coin calls
- Copying someone's portfolio selectively

**What makes a real strategy:**
- Predefined entry criteria
- Predefined exit criteria (thesis invalidation + thesis completion)
- Asymmetric upside built into the entry rule
- "A strategy is the principles and system you create that give you asymmetric upside"

## The Instructor's Four Edges

1. **Hunter Algorithm** — buy signal: price below moving average + negative funding rate (both required simultaneously)
2. **Presale deal flow** — access through private syndicate; do deals during bear markets so tokens unlock during bull runs
3. **Automated airdrop farming** — low cost, high wallet count automation at scale
4. **100x altcoin investing** — fundamental analysis framework for 5-100M market cap projects

## What Makes an Algorithm Real vs Fake

**Real algorithm:**
- Based on data that actually impacts market behavior (e.g., funding rate reflects crowd sentiment)
- Forward-tested with live signals (not just backtested)
- Wide parameter range that still produces results if numbers are adjusted slightly
- Does not reprint (signal does not change after 15 seconds)
- No alpha decay (data remains predictive regardless of how many people look at it)

**Fake algorithm (99% of them):**
- Built on arbitrary parameter combinations back-tested on historical data
- Anyone can back-test random variables to produce a seemingly accurate system
- Moving average crossovers = "the least reliable" algorithmic system
- Reprints signals retroactively as new data comes in

"Funding data is the only data set which doesn't suffer from alpha decay."

## Why Trading Fails (For 99.9%)

- Trading requires dedicating your **entire life** to it — no part-time profitable trader exists
- Requires experiencing a **full market cycle** firsthand — no shortcut
- Only caring about the **process**, never the outcome — emotional attachment = failure
- "If you don't like the idea of thinking about markets 24/7, don't try to trade"
- Cannot be copied — time-sensitive entries/exits mean followers consistently underperform

## Why You Cannot Copy-Trade Successfully

- Investing can be copied (time-insensitive entries/exits)
- Trading cannot be copied — by the time you act on a signal, the edge is gone
- You are consistently exiting later and with less profit, or selling after price has already dropped

## The Five Components of a Real Edge

1. **Real data** — not arbitrary back-tested parameters
2. **Confluence** — multiple independent data sources saying the same thing
3. **Intuition** — developed through thousands of hours of firsthand experience
4. **Network** — the people who help you build and refine the system
5. **Continuous refinement** — the system must be improved as markets evolve

## Key Warning: Avoid These "Strategies"

- Yield farming / staking / mining — "we're here for delta; the upside doesn't justify the risk of total loss"
- Copy-trading services — time-sensitivity kills the edge for followers
- Twitter/YouTube influencer calls — "influencers create liquidity, not alpha"
- Any algorithm without a live forward-tested track record
