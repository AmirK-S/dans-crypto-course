# The truth of being a profitable trader

**Section:** Building a Strategy/Edge
**Loom ID:** 79bbada89a7c4e1cb7352089d9592198

## Key Takeaways

- 99.9% of people who attempt trading fail to generate returns that justify the time invested — not because they lose all their money, but because the hourly return on thousands of hours of work is too low
- Trading is the hardest way to make money in existence — the competition includes AI systems with no emotions, professional market makers who manipulate price, and whale actors who bait retail into getting stopped out
- To be consistently profitable you must have experienced a full market cycle (bull, bear, and sideways) firsthand — each phase requires a completely different trading style, and you cannot learn this from someone else's experience
- There is no part-time profitable trader — every genuinely profitable trader interviewed dedicates their entire life to it: trading while at the gym, with their partner, while sleeping, at parties
- Profitable traders don't love the money or the thrill — they love the process of mastering a craft; those motivated by the outcome (wealth, freedom, dopamine) cannot maintain the required emotional detachment
- The darkest truth: if trading does make you rich, your dopamine receptors will be permanently damaged — you will never feel love, excitement, or happiness the same way again; it creates a form of financial PTSD
- Almost every trader on Twitter who appears profitable actually made their real money from long-term investments, not trading — spend 20% of your time on long-term investing for 80% of the results instead of the reverse
