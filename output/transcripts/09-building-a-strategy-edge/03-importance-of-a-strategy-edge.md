# Importance of a Strategy/Edge

**Section:** Building a Strategy/Edge

## Key Takeaways

- A strategy is not a plan ("I'll buy coins low and sell at the bull market top") — it is a specific, repeatable system with defined entry criteria, exit criteria, thesis validation/invalidation triggers, win rate, risk-reward ratio, and expected value
- Making money in markets requires becoming internationally competitive at crypto — the same as reaching national/international level in a sport; it requires mentors, good environment, a strategy, and a game plan
- Most people focus on finding the right coin as their strategy; the coin is actually the least important component — bad coins still go up 3x in a bull run, and you can triple your money on terrible coins with a great process
- Bad strategy hallmarks: following Twitter blindly, emotional decisions instead of process-based decisions, no defined exit criteria, no invalidation point, overfocusing on the plan not the system
- The Hunter Algorithm (the instructor's investing system) has an 84% win rate since 2019, using funding data + price below moving average as buy signals — this is an example of a real forward-tested system
- Invest using thesis-based exits not percentage stop losses — a coin going down 10% does not invalidate a thesis; you exit when the specific thesis conditions that made you buy are no longer valid
- The four edges used by the instructor: (1) Hunter algo long-term investing system; (2) presale access through private syndicates; (3) automated airdrop farming on 900+ wallets; (4) fundamental analysis framework for finding 5-100M market cap coins
