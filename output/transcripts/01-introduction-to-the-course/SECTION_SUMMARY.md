## 01-introduction-to-the-course — Trading Playbook Summary

### Core Concepts
- The core strategy is to use fundamental analysis to find small coins with 10–100x potential, buy them low, hold for multiple months (a year or more), and sell toward the end of the bull run
- The instructor frames the approach as holding "three to four, maybe five uncorrelated moon bags of small coins" and riding them for "ten to one hundred x or more gains instead of just trading your way to mental illness"
- The course is designed to teach first principles — the "raw ingredients" of investing — so students become independent analysts ("chefs") rather than just following step-by-step instructions
- The course is described as a jigsaw puzzle: individual sections (e.g. fundamental analysis alone) are incomplete without watching the whole thing in order
- The instructor outlines a three-cycle learning curve: first cycle = "monkey brain" buying hyped coins; second cycle = mid-curving into fundamentals but still underperforming; third cycle = blending both approaches and making serious money
- Paying "tuition to the market" (losing money, getting scammed, liquidated, or hacked) is presented as an unavoidable part of becoming a profitable investor — no course can remove this entirely, only speed up the process and reduce losses
- Tax and banking infrastructure for handling seven figures is flagged as part of the overall strategy

### Rules
- Watch the course in order: "introduction first... then start from the mindset and work your way down" — the order exists for a reason
- Do not skip sections: watching only one part (e.g. fundamental analysis) leaves you with "the corner of the jigsaw puzzle" and no full picture
- Understand first principles rather than just following a recipe: "I'm going to turn you into a chef... I'm going to teach you the first principles of the raw ingredients"
- Accept that losses are mandatory: "your tuition fee must be paid to the market... it is impossible that you become consistently profitable without losing heaps of money"
- Do not use high leverage: the instructor calls out a "guy doing 100x leverage — oh but only on a small amount of my money — just dumb"

### Key Warnings / What Not To Do
- Do not use high leverage: "a lot of you are gonna have to get liquidated with leverage... 100x leverage... just dumb"
- Do not expect a course to make you instantly profitable or to prevent losses: "to think you're just going to make money and never lose money again because you have a $1,000 course... is just not reality"
- Do not skip sections or watch out of order
- Do not buy coins purely because they are up significantly in a short period ("you just buy things that are up 50x in a day — you're a monkey brain")
- Be aware of scams, rug pulls, and hacks: "you're gonna have to get scammed, lose all your money in a rug pull... get hacked... no course, group or mentor can save you from this"
