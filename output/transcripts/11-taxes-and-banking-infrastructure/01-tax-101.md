## Key Takeaways

- There are three types of tax systems: citizenship-based (US taxes you wherever you live; requires renouncing citizenship to escape), residency-based (UK, Europe, Australia, Canada — taxes based on where you live), and territorial (only taxes locally-sourced income; foreign income exempt)
- The 183-day rule is a common guideline but not a hard rule — tax residency is determined by a combination of factors including where your permanent home is, bank accounts, family ties, and time spent; spending under 183 days somewhere is not a guarantee of avoiding tax residency
- Company tax residency can differ from personal tax residency — a major mistake is setting up a Dubai company while living in the UK, then continuing to run the company from the UK; management rules deem the company taxable where it is managed from, not where it is incorporated
- Countries with 0% crypto tax for individual investors: Dubai (UAE), Cyprus, Cayman Islands, Portugal (investors only), El Salvador, Malta, Panama, Germany (if held 1+ year) — always verify that your activity qualifies as "investing" and not "trading"
- If you stand to make 6+ figures in a bull run, setting up an offshore structure is a no-brainer: a $100k gain saves $20-30k in taxes; $500k saves $100-150k; $10M saves $2-3 million
- Australia and Canada have exit taxes that apply to crypto — if you're from these countries, you need to leave before the bull run peak, not after, or you'll pay tax on unrealized gains at the time of departure
- A proper offshore setup costs $5-25k and takes 1-3 months to implement; it requires choosing a target country, getting residency/visa, setting up personal banking, and registering on exchanges using new country credentials
