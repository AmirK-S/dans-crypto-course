# Taxes and Banking Infrastructure — Cheat Sheet

## The Three Tax Systems

| System | How It Works | Examples |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Citizenship-based | Taxed no matter where you live | USA (taxes all citizens globally) |
| Residency-based | Taxed based on where you are resident | UK, Europe, Canada, Australia |
| Territorial | Only local income taxed; foreign income exempt | Costa Rica, Thailand, Panama |

## The 183-Day Rule — A Guide, Not a Rule

- 183 days is a commonly cited threshold — but it is a **guideline, not a hard rule**
- Countries also look at: permanent home, family ties, bank accounts, domicile indicators
- "It is possible to spend 5 months in a country per year but have tax residency there" based on other ties
- The most secure setup: live in one low-tax country for the majority of the year — minimal room for judgment

## Crypto Tax Treatment

- **Investing** = capital gains tax (typically 20-30% in Western countries)
- **Trading** = personal income tax (typically 25-50%) — every swap or trade is a taxable event
- **Key rule:** Swapping BTC to ETH or BTC to USDC = selling your position = taxable event
- Countries offering 0% on crypto (e.g., Portugal, Germany) usually require **buy and hold for at least 1 year with no swapping**
- "The blockchain is literally a paper trail that can't be edited" — governments have Chainalysis relationships

## Countries with 0% Crypto Tax (Personal Investors)

| Country | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Dubai (UAE) | 0% capital gains on all assets including crypto; great banking infrastructure |
| Cyprus | Crypto tax exempt; good for 60-day residency; weak banking — have other accounts |
| Cayman Islands | Classic tax haven; 0% on everything |
| Portugal | Treats crypto as currency, not asset; 0% on increase in value |
| El Salvador | Bitcoin legal tender; 0% on BTC appreciation |
| Malta | 0% on crypto; good for European business (5% effective corporate rate) |
| Germany | Hold 1+ year = 0% personal crypto tax |

**Critical:** Must buy assets AFTER establishing tax residency in the 0% country AND sell while still resident.

## Management Rules — Offshore Company Warning

Setting up a Dubai company while living in the UK does **not** make the company tax-free in the UK.

- Company tax residency follows where it is **managed and controlled**, not where it is registered
- The offshore structure does not eliminate tax liability if you continue to manage it from your home country
- "Just because you keep the money in a foreign bank account does not avoid this"
- Nominee directors do not change this: "Steve thinks he's beating the system... he isn't"

## Pass-Through Entities (US LLCs, UK LLPs)

- Can be 0% tax IF there are **no local operations**
- No local operations = no employees, no freelancers, no office, no warehouse, no personal work from that country
- If you manage a US LLC from the UK while living in the UK → UK tax applies

## Exit and Departure Taxes

- Countries like **Australia and Canada** have exit taxes that apply to crypto
- These tax you on the **unrealized value** of your crypto at the point you formally leave as a tax resident
- If you wait until the bull run peak to leave → you pay tax on peak crypto values before you've even sold
- "If your country has a departure or exit tax you need to be looking at leaving now"
- After leaving a high-tax country, you may need to stay away for **5 years** before selling crypto bought before you left

## Banking Infrastructure — Non-Negotiable

- **Multiple exchange accounts** with multiple off-ramp options (in case one locks you during a bull run)
- Exchange accounts registered to **new country details** (residence card, new address, new bank)
- Keep crypto **off exchanges** — FTX was "one of the most reliable exchanges" before it went bust
- Avoid Cyprus as primary banking: government seized deposits over 100k euros in 2013
- Test the full chain: crypto → exchange → business bank → personal bank — before you need it

## Crypto Bought Personally vs Through Business

- Almost always better to **buy crypto personally**, not through a business
- Most 0% crypto tax countries apply only to **personal investors**, not corporate holdings
- "Investing in crypto via business isn't 0% tax on crypto whilst investing personally is"
- Exception: some people set up entities solely for asset protection — complex and requires professional advice

## Process for Going Offshore

1. Check for exit/departure tax in your current country
2. Identify preferred 0% crypto tax country (lifestyle first, then tax)
3. Apply for relevant visa or residency permit
4. Set up personal banking in new country (multiple accounts, crypto-friendly banks)
5. Register on crypto exchanges with new country details
6. Buy new crypto assets only after establishing residency
7. Sell only while still resident in the 0% country

**Cost:** $5,000-$25,000 depending on complexity. Timeline: 1-3 months. Get professional advice — "the worst thing to do is accidentally commit tax evasion."
