## Key Takeaways

- Crypto investing is typically taxed at capital gains rates (20-30% in most Western countries); crypto trading is typically taxed at higher personal income rates (25-50%) because each swap or trade is a taxable event
- Every time you swap one coin for another — not just when you withdraw to fiat — it is a taxable event in most countries; countries like Portugal and Germany that offer 0% crypto tax require you to hold positions for at least a year without swapping
- The power of tax deferral compounds significantly: a person who holds without swapping versus a frequent trader faces dramatically different effective tax rates over a bull run; frequent trading creates compounding tax drag
- Crypto is completely trackable via blockchain — the paper trail from your exchange account to bank account is permanent and tax authorities have relationships with Chainalysis, banks, and exchanges to access this data; the assumption that crypto is anonymous is dangerous
- The break-even case for going offshore: if you make $100k this bull run, you save $20-30k in taxes; if you make $500k, you save $100-150k; if you make $10M, you save $2-3M — the setup cost is trivially small relative to the savings at any of these levels
- Business considerations: investing in crypto through your company is usually not recommended — most 0% crypto tax countries only apply to personal investors, not corporate holdings; personal buying is generally more tax-efficient
- To qualify for 0% tax on crypto, you must buy assets after establishing tax residency in the low-tax country and sell them while still resident there — buying in a high-tax country and then moving does not retroactively exempt those gains
