## Key Takeaways

- Dubai full-time setup: 9% corporate tax on profits over 100k AED, 0% personal and capital gains tax; requires formally leaving your home country's tax residency and spending most of the year in Dubai — provides a legitimate, clear-cut offshore structure
- Dubai 90-day setup: you can establish Dubai tax residency by spending as little as 90 days per year there; Canadians and Australians must plan their exit tax carefully before leaving since both countries tax crypto at exit
- Cyprus 60-day setup: Cyprus offers tax residency after just 60 days per year if you have a company there; non-domiciled residents pay 0% on dividends (Malta has a similar remittance-based system); corporate tax is 12.5%, effective rate often lower
- The most common offshore mistake: setting up a company in Dubai (or Hong Kong) while still living full-time in your home country — management rules mean the company is taxed in your home country regardless of where it is incorporated
- US LLC and UK LLP pass-through structures: can be 0% corporate tax if there are no local operations (no employees, offices, or personal work done there); commonly used as payment processing entities feeding into a lower-tax holding structure
- Territorial tax countries (Costa Rica, Thailand) appear attractive but are often misunderstood: if you are the sole director running an offshore company from within the country, that income is no longer "foreign sourced" and becomes locally taxable under management rules
- The safest offshore structures involve physically living in one low-tax country for the majority of the year — strategies that depend on spending no time anywhere or stacking jurisdictional gray areas carry higher legal risk and require more sophisticated planning
