# Second and Third Order Consequences

**Section:** Mindset
**Loom ID:** d4c9b9e135c2436c8899376cdc989b78

## Key Takeaways

- Second and third order consequence thinking means analyzing what an action causes, then what that cause causes — the big money in markets is made from predicting two or three steps ahead.
- Most market participants only think one step ahead; finding a high-probability multi-step scenario that very few people have identified is how you generate outsized returns.
- The Toshi thesis illustrates this: Coinbase wins → sends users to Base blockchain → users buy the top meme coin (Toshi) — a third-order consequence most people hadn't spotted.
- The further ahead you can predict a scenario that eventually plays out, the more money you make — because you buy before anyone believes in it and sell when everyone does.
- To assess probability of a multi-step thesis, ask "how could this go wrong?" — if the failure scenarios are very unlikely and the success scenario is very likely, it's a strong thesis.
- Playing games like chess builds the mental skill of second and third order consequence thinking — chess players think five to fifteen moves ahead.
