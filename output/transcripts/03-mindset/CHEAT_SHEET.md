# Mindset — Cheat Sheet

## Crypto Is a Zero-Sum / Negative-Sum Game

- Every dollar you make comes from another participant's loss — this is not a cooperative game
- After fees and spreads, the aggregate is **negative-sum** (the house always takes a cut)
- **PVP mode**: competing directly against other traders (meme coins, short-term trades)
- **PVE mode**: buying and holding quality assets, competing against inflation and opportunity cost
- Most retail investors are "exit liquidity" — they buy from insiders who bought early and are selling to them

## How Money Actually Flows

- Early buyers (insiders, VCs, presale participants) sell to later buyers at higher prices
- As price rises, sellers gain increasing power — the order book thins above price
- Understanding bid/ask mechanics: bids are buyers waiting below price, asks are sellers waiting above
- Recognizing when you are the exit liquidity is a critical skill

## Emotional Control

- Emotions are the #1 killer of crypto returns — fear and greed cause buying high and selling low
- **FOMO** (Fear of Missing Out) drives buying at tops; **panic** drives selling at bottoms
- Develop rules in advance and follow them mechanically during volatile periods
- The market is designed to shake out weak hands — emotional reactions are exploited

## Pattern Recognition Over Predictions

- Successful investing is about **recognizing recurring patterns**, not predicting the future
- The goal is to position yourself before a pattern plays out, then let it unfold
- No one can predict tops and bottoms — focus on probability, not certainty
- Study past cycles to understand what tends to happen, then apply that probabilistically

## Preparedness, Not Predictions

- Have a **pre-made plan** for multiple scenarios before they happen
- "What will I do if X goes up 3x from here?" — decide now, not in the moment
- "What will I do if the market drops 50%?" — decide now, not during the crash
- Pre-commitment removes emotion from high-stakes decisions

## The "Tuition" Concept

- Losses in crypto are **tuition** — the cost of an education you can't get elsewhere
- Every major mistake teaches something that makes you more profitable long-term
- The question is not "did I lose money?" but "what did I learn, and was it worth the tuition?"
- Accept that early losses are part of the process; catastrophic losses are not

## Conviction and Position Sizing

- **High conviction = larger position** — size reflects your confidence in the thesis
- Low conviction plays should be small enough that losing them doesn't hurt
- Never bet so much that a single loss destroys your portfolio or your psychology
- The goal is to stay in the game long enough to hit the big winners

## Key Mindset Rules

1. Know which mode you are in (PVP vs PVE) before entering a position
2. Decide your exit criteria before you enter — not after
3. Treat losses as tuition, not failures
4. Never make decisions during peak emotional states (extreme greed or extreme fear)
5. Recognize that markets are adversarial — other participants want to take your money
