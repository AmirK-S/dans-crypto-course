# Dunning Kruger Effect

**Section:** Mindset
**Loom ID:** 32fe698287104d24b35a0c4dbe85a882

## Key Takeaways

- The Dunning-Kruger Effect means investors peak in confidence at the start (least knowledge) and hit their lowest confidence mid-journey — before eventually becoming competent.
- 95% of investors do not make money in their first market cycle; the most common experience is paying a big tuition fee to the market before the second cycle.
- You can reduce the chance of losing everything in the first cycle by working with a pro who knows when to sell, following proven strategies, and investing early in the bull run.
- Pay your tuition fee early and cheap: when you are young and have less money, the cost of mistakes is lower than paying it later when you have more to lose.
- You must make every mistake possible during your first market cycle — 80% of the required lessons can only be learned through firsthand experience, not from courses.
- In your first market cycle, never invest more than you're willing to lose — you are most mistake-prone and least experienced when capital at risk is highest.
