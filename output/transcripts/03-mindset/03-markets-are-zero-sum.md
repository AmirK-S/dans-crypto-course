# Markets are Zero Sum

**Section:** Mindset
**Loom ID:** 13f9e28d4c434a878b8416bd8c364af3

## Key Takeaways

- Every dollar you make in a market is a dollar someone else lost — markets are a closed pool of capital circulating between participants, not a system that creates new wealth; value is distributed, not created
- In practice, markets are negative-sum (not zero-sum) because exchanges and platforms extract fees on every transaction — you put in $100 and even in the best case can never get $100 back without taking it from someone else
- There are two phases in crypto markets: PVP (player vs player) during bear markets, where existing participants compete and the smart ones barely survive; and PVE (player vs enemy) during bull markets, where new retail entrants provide "exit liquidity" and experienced holders can all profit simultaneously
- Exit liquidity is the most profitable period in crypto — normies enter because of media hype, having no experience with the volatility or market cycles, and they fund the gains of everyone who survived the bear market; your goal is to sell to them
- The bidder/asker visualization: when bids (buy orders) are abundant and asks (sell orders) are thin, price is set to go up; when asks dominate and bids are thin, the top is in — order book depth data makes this visible in real time
- Making money in your first market cycle is extremely rare and may be harmful, because you cannot learn the lessons of not losing everything until you have lost everything; the inevitable tuition payment accelerates learning
- As sellers profit and prices rise, sellers gain proportionally more power (their holdings are worth more); as price falls, buyers gain relative power because sellers have less clout — this is why lower prices are structurally more bullish
