# Short Term vs Long Term Solutions

**Section:** Mindset
**Loom ID:** ab89ff3b90bb4d29a411cc555e82f058

## Key Takeaways

- When you make a mistake in crypto, there are two types of solutions: short-term (fix the immediate problem) and long-term (fix the root cause so it never happens again).
- Most investors only apply short-term solutions — they ask "how do I get into this coin I missed?" instead of "why did I miss it and how do I fix my system?"
- Pulling the weed from the surface (short-term fix) means the problem grows back; pulling from the root (long-term fix) removes the problem for the rest of your investing life.
- The right questions to ask after a mistake: Why did this happen? What went wrong in my system? Who did I listen to that I shouldn't have? What change prevents this from happening again?
- You need both: stop the immediate bleeding (short-term fix) AND put foam on the wall (long-term fix) — most people only do the first and never the second.
- Every common mistake in crypto — missing the bottom, buying too high, bag holding — has the same root cause: a flaw in your process that must be fixed systematically.
