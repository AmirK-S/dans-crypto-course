# Preparedness, not Predictions

**Section:** Mindset
**Loom ID:** b13326a2c0844ec0bf394402f2370f4b

## Key Takeaways

- Price predictions are useless because there are millions of variables and humans massively overestimate their ability to predict which one outcome will occur.
- Setting a price target removes fluidity from your investing — the market does not care where you bought or sold, and arbitrary targets cause you to miss actual exits.
- The best investors build a validation and invalidation thesis for the asset they buy, then enter when upside is asymmetric and exit when the thesis has played out.
- "Invest in preparedness, not in prediction" — be prepared for all possible scenarios and ready to act when the thesis is either validated or invalidated.
- You buy when the coin is not a consensus bet and no one is talking about it, then wait for your thesis to play out — not at any specific price target.
- The invalidation of the thesis is as important as the validation: if the key catalysts you expected don't materialize, that is your signal to exit.
