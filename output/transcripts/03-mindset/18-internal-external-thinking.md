# Internal —> External Thinking

**Section:** Mindset
**Loom ID:** 39cf116b94f749d68d8ee3f99c06d69e

## Key Takeaways

- Internal-to-external thinking means forming your own thesis first and then checking what others think — the wrong way is going external-first (copying Twitter/YouTube gurus) and then deciding internally.
- 90% of crypto investors outsource their conviction to someone they've never met on the internet — this leads to buying at the top because influencers gain conviction as price rises.
- You only safely copy someone if you can work one-on-one with them and get their exact exit signal — a Twitter guru tells you when they buy but never when they sell.
- The best trades are the ones where you share your thesis and receive the least amount of confirmation from others — that is the signal you are early.
- The correct process: research independently → build a thesis → find coins not a consensus bet → accumulate when upside is asymmetric → hold until thesis validated or invalidated.
- The less your thesis resonates with the masses today, the more future buyers exist if it plays out — that gap is where the profit lives.
