# MASTER SUMMARY: Bull Run Millions Crypto Course Trading Playbook

## Course Philosophy

Crypto markets are zero-sum (negative-sum once exchange fees are included): every dollar made is a dollar someone else lost. The only exception is the "exit liquidity" phase at the peak of a bull run, when new retail participants flood in and experienced holders can all profit simultaneously at the expense of the late arrivals. The instructor frames the entire course around this dynamic: surviving and accumulating during bear markets (PVP — player vs. player), then positioning early into asymmetric opportunities, and finally selling to the incoming wave of normies (PVE — player vs. enemy) before the cycle ends.

Markets repeat because humans repeat the same cognitive and emotional mistakes every four years. Profitable investing means recognizing those biases in yourself and doing the opposite of what instinct demands. The highest edge available to an independent investor is not information — it is emotional discipline combined with second- and third-order thinking that arrives at a thesis before the broader market understands it.

The core investment model is: use fundamental analysis to find small coins (5–100M market cap) with 10–100x potential, buy them during peak fear when perception is worse than reality, hold a concentrated portfolio of three to seven positions for the duration of a bull run thesis, and sell when the thesis plays out — i.e., when your intended exit liquidity (late retail) arrives. Tax and banking infrastructure to handle seven-figure outcomes is treated as an integral part of this plan, not an afterthought.

The course is described as a jigsaw puzzle — no single section is complete without the whole. Students are not given a recipe; they are taught first principles so they become independent analysts ("chefs") rather than followers.

## The Strategy in Brief

"Find and spot 10–100x opportunities, buy them low using a hands-off strategy, hold them through the course of a bull run, and then exit near the top."

More specifically: use fundamental analysis to identify new coins (under 12 months old, fair launch preferred, 5–100M market cap) whose perception is significantly worse than their reality, buy during peak fear when crypto Twitter has forgotten the coin and upside is asymmetric, hold a maximum of seven liquid positions (two to three long-term multi-cycle holds plus three to five shorter-cycle altcoins), and sell when the thesis plays out — when "your once third-order consequences become the market's front-facing reality" and the retail/normie wave arrives to be your exit liquidity. Earlier in the bull run, take on risk; later in the bull run, take it off.

## Entry Signals & Conditions

**Sentiment-based signals:**
- Buy when sentiment is at its worst and the most people hate a coin: "the best time to buy is when no one wants to buy." Look for the point where "there's no one left to hate, there's no one left to sell, there's no more bad news to come." [from Market Psychology]
- Enter a position "when the upside is asymmetric" and "when the coin is not a consensus bet / majority aren't interested." [from Mindset]
- "Your best investments will be the hardest to buy in the moment." If buying feels easy or comfortable, the upside is likely not sufficiently asymmetric. [from Mindset]
- Buy when crypto Twitter has forgotten about the coin and there is fear among holders. [from Crypto Cycles]
- Negative funding rate (crowd is overly short/bearish) historically coincides with market bottoms and is a signal to consider buying. Positive funding (crowd is overly long/bullish) historically coincides with tops and is a signal to consider selling. [from Crypto Cycles]

**Fundamental entry signals:**
- Outlier price action — a coin that does not go down when the rest of the market dumps, or goes up while Bitcoin makes lower lows. "If bitcoin dropping 10% in a day can't make a coin go down, what will?" Described as "one of the highest hit-rate setups in crypto." [from Fundamental Analysis]
- Bad news + price not going down — bearish news that fails to move price signals bad news is priced in and underlying strength exists. [from Fundamental Analysis]
- Perception significantly worse than reality — buy when the perceived value is materially lower than assessed reality. [from Fundamental Analysis]
- New coins on small exchanges (MEXC, ExBitX, KuCoin, SafeTrade) — new layer 1 and AI coins listed on smaller exchanges first; screen for outlier price action in early months. [from Fundamental Analysis]
- CoinGecko top gainers during market dumps — screening top gainers in the top 1,000 coins during a broad selloff identifies coins with relative strength. [from Fundamental Analysis]
- Zero-to-one primitives in their first or second iteration — the first good iteration of a genuinely new technology or sector. [from Fundamental Analysis]
- Strong community + outlier price action + fair launch + new coin (under 12 months, no prior bull cycle). [from Fundamental Analysis]

**Technical entry signals:**
- Breakout and retest: price breaks above a key resistance level on high volume, then retests that level as support. Buy on the retest. [from Technical Analysis]
- Sizable dip / retracement buy after a coin has pulled back by its historically typical percentage:
  - Bitcoin: ~20% pullback in a bull market [from Technical Analysis]
  - Ethereum: ~25% pullback in a bull market [from Technical Analysis]
  - Solana: ~30–50% pullback in a bull market [from Technical Analysis]
  - Meme coins: ~40–60% pullback in a bull market (sometimes 70%) [from Technical Analysis]
- Bear market accumulation: wait for altcoins to decline 80–90% before looking for entries. [from Technical Analysis]
- Capitulation buy (confluence of): hunter algorithm buy signal (negative funding + price below moving average), realized losses deeply negative, large long liquidations, spike in bid depth showing smart money accumulating, high DEX volume spikes, highly negative aggregate funding rates. All tools must be used together. [from Technical Analysis]

**For the Big Long (early bull, limited to top-tier analysts):**
- Enter after a key breakout from an accumulation region, then wait for a retest of a significant level ("dark green" zone). This only applies early in the bull run. [from Crypto Cycles]

**Hunter algorithm (long-term investing system):**
- Buy signal requires simultaneously: (1) price below a specific moving average AND (2) negative funding data. Wait 10–15 seconds after the daily candle open before acting. Additional confluence datasets checked after the signal determine confidence level and position size. [from Building a Strategy Edge]

**Pre-announcement positioning:**
- Buy before a major announcement, not after. Smart investors buy in anticipation of news and sell "three to seven days before the announcement comes out." [from Market Psychology]

## Position Sizing & Risk Management

**Portfolio structure:**
- Maximum of seven liquid positions: two to three long-term multi-cycle holds plus three to five shorter-cycle altcoin positions. [from Crypto Cycles]
- Concentrated bets: "There's only 2 to 3 really really good high conviction bets" at any time. [from Do-Not-Do List]
- Capital allocation by portfolio size [from Building a Strategy Edge]:
  - Under $2,000: focus entirely on airdrops and sub-$100M market cap coins with 20x potential.
  - $2,000–$15,000: same focus, with free money opportunities added.
  - $15,000–$50,000: half in high-upside altcoins, with presales and free money opportunities.
  - $15,000–$200,000: drop manual airdrop farming, focus on presales and long-term investments.
  - Over $200,000: barbell strategy (mostly safe/major assets, 10–30% high-upside smaller projects); no manual airdrop farming.

**Sizing and adding:**
- Size positions to a level where a 50% loss would not cause you to act emotionally. [from Mindset]
- Position sizing is influenced by the level of confluence at the time of a signal — more confirming data points warrant a larger position. [from Building a Strategy Edge]
- Never increase position sizing as the portfolio grows later in the bull run. [from Crypto Cycles]
- Do not add more capital to the market as the bull run progresses — "you should be reducing your risk, not increasing it." [from Do-Not-Do List]
- "You always want to have a little bit more than what feels comfortable" in terms of position size — meaningful but not so large it creates emotional decisions. [from Do-Not-Do List]

**Exits and stop losses:**
- The invalidation of the thesis is the stop loss — not a price level or percentage. [from Fundamental Analysis, Technical Analysis, Building a Strategy Edge]
- Sell when: (1) the thesis plays out, (2) the thesis is invalidated, or (3) a clearly better opportunity exists. [from Do-Not-Do List]
- Sell when your intended exit liquidity (late retail / normies) arrives — triggered by the arrival of the target buyer demographic, not a price. [from Fundamental Analysis]
- Do not sell half on a 2x — described as "one of the dumbest strategies" that cuts winners early and violates the asymmetric return model. [from Do-Not-Do List]
- Selling and re-buying increases decisions (each added decision is added risk), taxes, and the risk of being forced into laggards. [from Do-Not-Do List]

**Expected return ranges (for calibrating when to reduce risk):**
- Bitcoin: 4–8x from cycle bottom to top [from Technical Analysis]
- Ethereum: similar to Bitcoin, slightly more [from Technical Analysis]
- Solana: 15–30x from cycle bottom to top [from Technical Analysis]
- Meme coins: 10–100x from cycle bottom to top [from Technical Analysis]

**During bear markets:**
- Capital preservation is the explicit priority. "When the market is in a bear market, the key is to preserve your capital." Losing 80% during a bear market means 80% less capital to deploy in the next opportunity. [from Crypto Cycles]
- Reduce leverage and overall risk exposure as the bull run matures. During the final phase, move predominantly into stablecoins. [from Crypto Cycles]

**Yield / risk-reward framing:**
- "You want to be making 10 reward for every [1 risk]" as a general principle. [from Scams to Avoid]
- Yield farming risk-reward: "you potentially lose 100% if something goes wrong and make what, 20%, 50% — so your risk to reward is 0.5 risk for one reward... that's a bad ratio." [from Scams to Avoid]

**Tax-aware holding:**
- Holding fewer positions for as long as possible is better from a tax compounding perspective. [from Taxes and Banking]
- In Australia: holding under one year triggers short-term capital gains tax significantly higher than long-term capital gains tax for positions held over one year. [from Do-Not-Do List]

## Section-by-Section Quick Reference

**01 — Introduction to the Course:** Frames the course as a jigsaw puzzle of first principles, not a recipe; the strategy in brief is to find small coins with 10–100x potential, hold three to five concentrated positions through the bull run, and sell near the top. Most actionable takeaway: watch every section in order — skipping any part leaves you with a corner of the puzzle.

**03 — Mindset:** Covers the zero-sum nature of crypto markets, the PVP/PVE framework, emotional discipline, tuition payments, and the thesis-based (not price-target-based) approach to investing.
